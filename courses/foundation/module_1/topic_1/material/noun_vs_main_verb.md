##Noun and Main Verb: An Introduction

### Round 1: The Noun
Nouns are the *names* of people, places, things, or ideas.

### Round 2: The Main Verb
Main verbs are the *action words*.



### Nouns and Verbs: Grocery Store Adventure!
Imagine going on a fun **grocery store adventure** where you'll learn about **nouns** and **verbs** while filling your **shopping cart** with goodies.

### Round 1: The Noun Expedition

**Nouns** are the **names** of **things**, and they are highlighted here in **bold**.

- **Fruits** and **Vegetables**: Think of colorful nouns like **apple**, **banana**, **carrot**, and **broccoli**.
- **Dairy Section**: You'll find nouns like **milk**, **cheese**, and **yogurt**.
- **Snacks** and **Treats**: The **snack aisle** is full of yummy nouns like **chips**, **cookies**, and **popcorn**.
- **Shopping Cart**: Your trusty **cart** will carry all your chosen nouns to the **checkout**.





###Round 2: The Verb Quest
**Verbs** are **action words**, and they are highlighted here in **bold**.

- **Picking**: **Pick** the freshest fruits and vegetables.
- **Grabbing**: **Grab** a gallon of milk and your favorite cheese from the dairy section.
- **Resisting**: **Use** willpower to **resist** the urge for cookies and **choose** a healthier snack.
- **Pushing**: **Push** your full cart through the store aisles.
- **Paying**: Finally, **pay** for your groceries before heading home.

##Homonyms

Words that have the same spelling and pronunciation but have different meanings.

####Examples: 
- `Bear (animal) and Bear (to tolerate)`
- `Bank (financial institution) Bank (side of a river)`
- `Right (correct) Right (opposite to left)`

##Homophones
Words that have the same pronunciation but have different spelling and meanings.

####Examples:
- `Rice (a type of grain) and Rise (upward movement)`
- `Hair (strand on the body) and Hare (a fast-running mammal)`
- `One (the number 1) and Won (past tense of win)`
