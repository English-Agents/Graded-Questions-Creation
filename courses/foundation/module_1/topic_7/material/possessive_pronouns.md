# Possessive Pronouns

##Pronouns: Possessive

####List of Possessive pronouns:


| Noun                        | Pronouns for belongings     | Example 1                             | Example 2                               |
|-----------------------------|--------------|---------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------|
| Something belonging to the speaker                | *my, mine*       | My dog loves to play fetch.            | The red ball is mine.                   |
| Something belonging to the person whom the speaker is talking to| *your, yours*         | Is that your backpack?              | The bicycle is yours.        |
| Other person's belongings (male)                     | *his*      | His favorite color is blue.                     | That is his notebook.                      |
| Other person's belonging (female)                   | *her, hers*     |Her book is on the table.                  | The jacket is hers.        |
| Something belonging to a group of people, animals or things             |*their, theirs*          | Their house is painted white.               |Those toys are theirs.                            |
| Something belonging to a group including the speaker (We)     |*our, ours*      | Our family enjoys hiking together.       | The car is ours.           |
| Something belonging to a thing or animal               | *its*  | The cat is cleaning its fur. |The computer is slow because its memory is nearly full.        |



##Pronouns: Point out

###Point out pronouns are four in number.

```
For singular nouns----this, that
For plural nouns------these, those
```



| Nouns                          | Corresponding Pronouns |
| ------------------------------ | ---------------------- |
| Something (one) near           |  this                |
| Something (one) away           |  that                |
| Somethings (many) near         |  these               |
| Somethings (many) away         |  those               |



#####Examples
```
That boy
These shoes
This pen
Those people
```



##Application of Pronouns in Statements

-  *Those* groceries I bought at the store are *mine*.
- You should take care of *your* well-being and prioritize self-care.
- He is an excellent musician, and *his* talent shines through *his* performances.
- It is raining outside, so do not forget to bring *your* umbrella.
- *We* are going on a vacation next week, and *our* excitement is noticeable.
- *They* are *my* best friends, and I value *their* friendship immensely.
- The house belongs to *them*, and *they* take pride in maintaining *its* beautiful garden.
- The dog wagged *its* tail, happy to see *its* owner after a long day.
- In the photo, *I* am standing next to *my* sister, and *our* smiles reflect the joy *we* felt in that moment.
- *He* asked *me* if *I* could lend *him* *my* pen, and *I* gladly handed it over.
- *She* expressed *her* gratitude for the support *she* received from *her* friends during a difficult time.
- *They* are the winners of the competition, and *their* hard work and dedication paid off.
- Look at *that* magnificent sunset; *it* is truly a sight to behold.
- The book *you* borrowed from the library is *mine*; please remember to return *it* soon.
- The cake on the table is *ours*, so feel free to have a slice.
