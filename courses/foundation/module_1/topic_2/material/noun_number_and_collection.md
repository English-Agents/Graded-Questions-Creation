#Noun: Number and Collection
###Number - **Singular and Plural**

- Singular = One
**Examples:** Paper, Mango, Opportunity,Boy, Analysis, Monkey, Knife etc.

- Plural = Many
**Examples:** Papers, Mangoes, Opportunities, Boys, Analyses, Monkeys, Knives etc.

###More Examples of Singular and Plural nouns

| Singular         | Plural        |
|------------------|---------------|
| Man              | Men           |
| Mouse            | Mice          |
| Tooth            | Teeth         |
| Foot             | Feet          |
| Focus            | Foci          |

| Curriculum       | Curricula     |
| Crisis           | Crises        |
| Diagnosis        | Diagnoses     |
| Criterion        | Criteria      |
`

**Exception**

```
For some words, both the singular and plural remain the same.

Examples: 

Sheep- Sheep
Deer- Deer
Series- Series
Aircraft- Aircraft
Species- Species
Swine- Swine
```
####A Point to Remember
DO NOT USE APOSTROPHE (’) WITH S IN PLURAL NOUNS

#####Examples: 
```
My friend's ❌           My friends ✅
Green vegetable's ❌     Green vegetables ✅
Boy's ❌                 Boys ✅ 
```

##Collection
**Definition:** a word that refers to a group of people, animals, or things **considered as a single unit**.

####Examples
```
Student = singular noun
Students = plural noun
Class = collective noun 
```
```
Flower = singular noun
Flowers = plural noun
Bunch =   collective noun
```
```
Player = singular noun
Players = plural noun
Team = collective noun
```


**A Gentle Reminder:** Though they consist of multiple things, the collective nouns are singular.

#####Examples:
```
The team is playing well.
The class has a test tomorrow.
```
###More examples of Collective nouns
```
A pack of cards
A flock of birds
A pair of shoes
A board of directors 
A bunch of flowers
A swarm of bees
A pile of papers
A heap of garbage
A team of players
```
