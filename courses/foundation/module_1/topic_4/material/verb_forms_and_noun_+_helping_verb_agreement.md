# Verb Forms and Noun + Helping Verb Agreement

#Reading Material Part 1 

##Actions and Its Different Forms

**Verbs** are like the action stars of sentences. They tell you what's happening or what someone or something is doing.



##Verb Forms
###Five forms in total
- **Present/Root Form (1st Form):**
  - Go
  - Eat
  - Play
  - Write
  - Sing

- **Past Form (2nd Form):**
  - Went
  - Ate
  - Played
  - Wrote
  - Sang

- **Past Participle Form (3rd Form):**
  - Gone
  - Eaten
  - Played
  - Written
  - Sung

- **ING Form (1st Form + -ing):**
  - Going
  - Eating
  - Playing
  - Writing
  - Singing
  
- **5th Form (1st Form + s/es)**:
  
 - Goes
 - Eats
 - Plays
 - Writes
 - Sings



| **Verb Forms**          | **Examples**                                                            |
|-------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| *1st Form*            | I eat pizza every Friday.                                               |
| *2nd Form*           | Yesterday, I ate a delicious pizza.                                     |
| *3rd Form*            | I have eaten pizza twice this week!                                     |
| *Verb + (ing)*        | I am eating pizza right now.                                            |
| *Verb + s/es*        | Every morning, Sarah eats a bowl of cereal before heading to work.      |

# Conversion from Positive to Negative Sentences Without Helping Verbs

## Steps for Conversion

1. **Look for Helping Verbs**  
   Check if there is a helping verb in the sentence.

2. **If there is no Helping Verb, find the Main Verb.**  
   Identify the main verb in the sentence.

3. **Identify the Main Verb Form**  
   Determine which form of the main verb is being used.

## Main Verb Forms and Their Conversion

### I Form (Base Form)
- **Rule:** Use "do not" + base form of the verb.
- **Example:**
  - **Positive:** "I call her."
  - **Negative:** "I do not call her."

### V Form (Verbs that end in -s or -es)
- **Rule:** Use "does not" + base form of the verb.
- **Example:**
  - **Positive:** "He calls her."
  - **Negative:** "He does not call her."

### II and III Forms (Past Simple and Past Participle)

- **II Form:**
  - **Example:**
    - **Positive:** "He called her."
    - **Negative:** "He did not call her."

- **III Form:**  
  The III form cannot exist without a helping verb.
  - **Example:**
    - **Positive:** "He has called her." (has is the helping verb)
    - Since the III form always has a helping verb, its conversion is not applicable in this context.

## Detailed Explanation

When converting positive sentences to negative sentences without helping verbs, the first step is to identify whether a helping verb is present. If there is no helping verb, focus on the main verb of the sentence.

Next, determine the form of the main verb:

- **I Form:** The base form of the verb is used with "do not."
  - **Example:** "I play" becomes "I do not play."

- **V Form:** For third person singular present tense, use "does not" + base form of the verb.
  - **Example:** "He plays" becomes "He does not play."

- **II Form:** For past simple tense, use "did not" + base form of the verb.
  - **Example:** "She walked" becomes "She did not walk."

**Remember:** The III form (past participle) cannot exist without a helping verb, so if you encounter a verb in this form, it indicates the presence of a helping verb.
