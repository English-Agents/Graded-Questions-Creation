# Possessive Noun: Apostrophe and Of Form

##Possessive Nouns
###Definition
####To mark belonging
####or
####To indicate ownership


###Sign of Possessive noun

####APOSTROPHE and s
#####combination of Apostrophe (') and s


####Examples

```
Rohan's cap
Seeta's house
Friends' plans
Employees' attendance
Developer's code
```


###How to apply Apostrophe

```
Possessive noun set
    /          \        
owner word     belonging word

e.g. candidate's resume
        |          |
 owner word      belonging word
 ```
 
###Key Rule for Apostrophe

1. Identify owner word and belonging word.
2. Check the form of the owner word:

   - If the owner word is singular, the apostrophe is put before 's'.
     - e.g.
       - Rohan's cap
       - Sneha's book
       - Child's toy

**(BECAUSE PLURAL WORD MOSTLY HAS AN 'S' IN THE END ALREADY).**

####For plural nouns that don’t end in ‘s’ (such as children, men, women, etc.), to create a possessive noun set, simply add      **apostrophe + s** (' + s) to the end.

#####Examples:

*Children’s*
*Women’s*
*Men’s*
*Feet’s*

####Check out these scenarios and see how possessive nouns level up your communication:

**The Mystery of the Missing Cookies**

**You:** "Someone ate the cookies' icing!"  
**Sibling:** "Maybe it was the dog's mischief. Check the kitchen's counter."

**The Smartphone Struggle**

**You:** "Hey, I think I left my phone at Sarah's house."  
**Friend:** “I don't have my phone handy. But, no worries! You can call from my friend's phone.”

```
Note: For proper nouns which end with 's', like James or Boris, 
you can either add an apostrophe (James') or add 's' after the apostrophe (James's).
```



#Possessive noun: Of form
###Apostrophe to of form and vice versa


 
###Some Examples:


- `Apostrophe form : Writer's pen`
`Of form  :  Pen of the writer`

- `Apostrophe form : Students' backpacks`
`Of form :  Backpacks of the students`

- `Apostrophe form : Bottles' caps`
`Of form : Caps of the bottles`
