# Pronouns: People and Things

##Pronouns: People & Things

###Pronouns

Pronouns are **words that take the place of nouns** in a sentence to avoid repetition.


**List of pronouns to denote people and things:**

- `I, me`
- `You`
- `He, him`
- `She, her`
- `It`
- `We, us`
- `They, them`



####Pronouns with Example:


| Number  | Nouns                                    | Corresponding Pronouns | Example                                                          |
|---------|------------------------------------------|------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Singular| When the speaker is speaking about themselves| I, me                  | - I wrote this message.
 - Could you send it to me?                     |
| Singular| A male                                   | He, him                | John arrived. Please tell him the news.                          |
| Singular  | When the speaker is addressing one person| You  (if focusing on the singular use)                  | You did a fantastic job!                                     |
| Singular| A female                                 | She, her               | Sarah excelled in this competition. Give the award to her.        |
| Singular| When a person's gender is unknown, unspecified, or non-binary (neither exclusively male nor female)                       | They, them             | The new employee is starting today. They will be joining the marketing team, so please introduce yourself to them.       |
| Singular| Thing or animal                          | It                     | The book is open. Close it.                                      |
|Plural        |When the speaker is addressing multiple people|        You|        You did a great job on the project! (Here, "you" refers to a group of people.)|
| Plural  | When referring to other people, things, or animals in a plural sense          | They, them             | The students were excited because they knew the teacher would congratulate them.              |
| Plural| The speaker along with other people    | We, Us                 | We are ready to begin. Join us.                                  |



####Some examples of the conversion of nouns into pronouns.


| Noun             | Corresponding Pronoun              |
|------------------|----------------------|
| *Sunil* wrote a code.       | *He* wrote a code.        |
| Could *Neha* bring me some water?  | Could *she* bring me some water?   |
| *My parents* bought a new house. | *They* bought a new house.     |
| The *movie* was fantastic.   | *It* was fantastic.           |

```
For animals you are familiar with, such as your pets or domesticated animals, 
you can use 'he' or 'she' depending on the context.
```
