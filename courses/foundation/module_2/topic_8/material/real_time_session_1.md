# Real Time Session 1

# Skit Activity Instructions  

**Introduction:**  
In this activity, you will work in groups to perform a skit based on a video. The goal is to improve your understanding of **characters, dialogues, teamwork, and creativity**. You will watch a video, take on character roles, and then prepare and present a short performance to the class.  

---

## Step 1: Form Groups  
- You will be divided into groups of **5 members**.  
- Each group will receive a **video link** to watch.  
- Here's the video link for the skit: <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Juzc0T3aO90" target="_blank">Skit Video Link</a>

   

---

## Step 2: Choose Characters  
- After watching the video, each group member will take on one character.  
- The characters are:  
  - Snail  
  - Jack  
  - Bobby  
  - Dury  
  - Chip  
- If a character has very few dialogues, the student playing that role may also take on the role of **Mom** so that everyone participates.  

---

## Step 3: Prepare Your Skit (20 minutes)  
- Watch the video carefully and note down the **exact dialogues**.  
- Assign lines to each group member according to their character.  
- Practice together and decide how to act out the scenes.  
- Help each other with **timing, body language, and expressions**.  

---

## Step 4: Perform Your Skit (3 minutes per group)  
- Each group will get **3 minutes** to present their skit.  
- Make sure you:  
  - Speak clearly and confidently.  
  - Use expressions and body language that match your character.  
  - Work together so the performance is smooth and fun.  

---

## Tips for Success  
-  **Work as a team:** Support each other with dialogues and expressions.  
-  **Use your time wisely:** Practice during the 20 minutes so you’re ready.  
-  **Stick to the script:** Use the key dialogues from the video.  
-  **Be creative:** Bring energy and life to your characters.  
-  **Stay focused:** Don’t get distracted during preparation.  

---

## Remember  
- Everyone must participate.  
- Stick to your role and be confident.  
- Most importantly → **Enjoy the activity and have fun!**  

---

## After the Performances  
- Once all groups have presented, there will be a short **feedback session** where the instructor shares what worked well and what can be improved.
