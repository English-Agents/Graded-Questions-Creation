# Articles: A / An and The

# Reading Material Part 1
### Articles
**Definition:**

A word which comes before a noun/ a combination of adjective and noun.

### Types of Articles

1. **Definite Article (The):**
The definite article "the" is used in English to refer to a specific noun that is identifiable to both the speaker and the listener. It indicates that the noun being mentioned is distinct and known within the context, either because it has been mentioned before or because it is commonly understood. "The" can be used with both singular and plural nouns, regardless of whether the noun starts with a consonant or vowel sound.

**Examples:**

- *The dog barked loudly.* (Refers to a specific dog that both the speaker and listener are aware of.)
- *I saw the car you were talking about.* (Refers to a particular car that has been previously mentioned or is understood from the context.)
- *The apples on the table are fresh.* (Refers to specific apples that are identifiable to both the speaker and listener.)
- *The sun rises in the east.* (In this sentence, "the sun" is commonly understood because there is only one sun that everyone knows about. The identity of "the sun" is clear to both the speaker and the listener, making it unnecessary to further specify which sun is being referred to.)







2. **Indefinite Articles (A, An):**
The indefinite articles 'a' and 'an' are used before singular nouns to refer to something non-specific or general, signaling that the noun represents any one of its kind rather than a particular item.

   *Example:* She adopted a cat." (Here, "a cat" is non-specific—any cat, not a particular one.)



### The Rule for Using "A" or "An"

The rule for using "a" or "an" before a word is based on the initial sound of the word, not just the first letter:

- **Use "a"** before words that start with a consonant sound.
  - **Example:** a book, a car, a university (because "university" starts with a "yu" sound, which is a consonant sound).

- **Use "an"** before words that start with a vowel sound.
  - **Example:** an apple, an hour (because "hour" starts with a silent "h," making the first sound a vowel).

 
### How to Apply Articles: A/An

 - **Check the first letter** of nouns, or in the case of adjective-noun combinations, check the first letter of the adjective.

 - **If the first letter begins with a vowel sound, use "an."**  
   "An" is also used with abbreviations that start with a vowel sound.  
   
   **Examples:**
   - *An individual*
   - *An enchanting fortress*
   - *An MLA*

3. **If the first letter has a consonant sound, use "a."**  

   **Examples:**
   - *A horse*
   - *A BMW car*

#Reading Material Part 2
## Article: The

*Goes with all the specific nouns.*

### Specific

1. **Absolute specific**
   - *The sun*
   - *The Eiffel tower*
   - *The United States Constitution*

2. **Context-based specific**
   - *The book on the table*
   - *The person I met yesterday*
   - *The car in the driveway*
   

###No article rule
- *Names of anything*
- *Plurals in a general sense*
- *Uncountable nouns*
- *Feelings and emotions*
- *Academic subjects, sports, languages*
- *Fixed phrase*
