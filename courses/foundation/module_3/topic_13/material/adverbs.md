# Adverbs

##Adverbs
Adverbs are words that describe verbs, adjectives or adverbs.

**Here's the simple scoop:**

*Adverbs tell you more about verbs, adjectives and adverbs. They answer questions like "how?" "when?" and "where?"*

```
Example: The bird sang beautifully.
Example: He recently moved to a new city. 
Example: She sat down.
Example: Ria wore a very beautiful dress.
Example: They were moving too slowly for the project to finish on time.
```

#Key takeaways:

#### Types of Adverbs:
- **Adverbs of manner**: Describe *how* something is done (e.g., *quickly*, *carefully*).
- **Adverbs of place**: Describe *where* something happens (e.g., *here*, *there*).
- **Adverbs of time**: Describe *when* something happens (e.g., *yesterday*, *now*).
- **Adverbs of degree**: Describe *to what extent* (e.g., *very*, *almost*).

#### Placement of Adverbs:
- Adverbs can appear at the beginning, middle, or end of a sentence depending on what they modify (e.g., *She sings beautifully* or *Suddenly, the rain stopped*).

#### Modifying Words:
- Adverbs can modify verbs (*runs quickly*), adjectives (*very tall*), or other adverbs (*quite slowly*).

#### Not All Adverbs End in -ly:
- Common adverbs like *here*, *now*, *never*, and *well* don’t follow the *-ly* pattern.

#### Adverbs and Sentence Clarity:
- The correct use of adverbs can enhance clarity and precision in communication by answering questions like *how*, *when*, *where*, or *to what extent*.

#### Adverbs and Adjectives:
Be careful not to confuse adverbs with adjectives. Adjectives modify nouns, while adverbs modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs (e.g., He is a good singer vs. He sings well).
