# Tenses: Possession (Past, Present, and Future)

Understanding possession in different tenses helps us express **when something belongs to someone** in the past, present, or future. This guide explores how to use words to show possession in each tense, using real-time scenarios and conversations. Both **positive** and **negative** forms of sentences are covered for possession in the present, past, and future.

---

## 1. Present Possession

When talking about something that someone **has right now**, we use the present tense. The key words are "have" and "has."

### Positive Sentences

#### How to Use "Have" and "Has"
- **Have** is used with *I,* *you (singular and plural),* *we,* *they,* *these,* *those,* or *any plural noun*.
- **Has** is used with *he,* *she*, *it*, *this*, *that*, or *any singular noun*.





```
Examples

- I have a sister.
- He has an interesting book collection.
- We have enough time to finish our project.
```






###Real-Time Scenarios
- *In a classroom:* The students **have** their books open on their desks.
- *At home:* Mom **has** a recipe book in the kitchen.





###Conversation Examples
>**A:** Do you **have** the car keys?  
  >**B:** Yes, I **have** them in my pocket.
  

  
>**A:** Does Tina **have** her phone with her?  
  >**B:** No, she left it at home.
  
------




### Negative Sentences

When talking about something that someone *does not have* right now, we use:

- **Do not have** (don't have) with *I*, *you* (singular and plural), *we*, *they*, *these*, *those*, or *any plural noun*.
- **Does not have** (doesn't have) with *he*, *she*, *it*, *this*, *that*, or *any singular noun*.



```
Examples:
- I don’t have any time to attend the meeting today.
- These books don't have any illustrations.
- This equipment doesn’t have any safety features.

```



###Real-Time Scenarios

- **In conversation:** "I *do not have* a car right now."
- **At work:** "She *doesn't have* any plans for the weekend."





###Conversation Examples
>**A:** Do you have a smartphone?  
 **B:** No, I **don't have** one. I'm planning to buy one soon.
 

 
>**A:** Does she **have** a pet at home?  
**B:** No, she **doesn't have** one anymore. She gave it to a friend.

----

## 2. Past Possession

When we talk about something that someone **owned or had in the past**, we use "had." Here, past refers to **any moment that has passed**. It could be years/ months/ weeks/ days or minutes before.


**Examples:**
I had a bike *when I was a child.*
He had a job in the city *two years ago*.
They had a beautiful garden *last summer*.
I had my keys in my hand just *a minute ago*.

--------

###(i) Positive Sentences

####How to Use "Had"
- **Had** is used for everyone, regardless of whether the noun is singular or plural.



```
Examples:
- She had a beautiful dress for the party.
- We had a beautiful house before moving to the city.
- They had a collection of rare books in their library.
```





#### Real-Time Scenarios
- *At school:* "Yesterday, I *had* my lunchbox, but I forgot it at home today."
- *During a conversation:* "They *had* a lovely dog when they lived in the countryside."






#### Conversation Examples
>**A:** Did you **have** a bicycle when you were a kid?  
  >**B:** Yes, I **had** a red one, but I outgrew it.
  

 
>**A:** I can't believe she forgot our meeting!
>**B:** I **had a feeling** this would happen.



--------




###(ii) Negative Sentences

When we talk about something that someone **did not have** in the past, we use "did not have."

#### Negative Form
- *"Did not have"* (didn't have) is used for everyone, no matter who or what is being talked about.

```
Examples:

- I did not have any siblings when I was growing up.
- She didn't have a job last year.
- They didn't have enough time to visit us yesterday.
```






####Real-time scenarios:

*A nostalgic memory:* As a child, I *didn't have* a smartphone, so I spent hours playing outside with my friends.

*At a restaurant:* Yesterday while we were eating, when it was time to pay the bill, I realised that I *didn't have* enough money to pay the bill, so I had to borrow some from a friend.




#### Conversation Example
 > **A:** I heard you *had* a big garden at your old house.  
> **B:** Yes, but we *didn't have* enough time to maintain it, so we had to sell it.



----


## 3. Future Possession

To talk about something that someone **will have** in the future, we use "will have."

-----



####(i) Positive Sentences

**How to Use "Will Have"**

- *"Will have"* is used when we are talking about `future events or actions`. It's a way to indicate that the possession is *not happening now*, but it **will happen** at a later time.
- *"Will have"* remains the same for everyone, no matter whether the noun is singular or plural.




**To better understand its usage, let's examine the following example:**


*"I will have a new phone next month."*

   **Meaning:** Currently, I do not own the new phone, but I expect to own it next month. Using "will have" indicates that this possession is planned for a future tiime.
   
   -----
   


```
More Examples:

- I will have my own car by next year.
- They will have a bigger office after the expansion.
- He will have a car when he turns 18.
```




**Conversation Examples**

>- **A:** Will you **have** enough money to buy the concert tickets?  
  **B:** Yes, I **will have** enough by the end of the week.
  

  
- **A:** Will they **have** a new house soon?  
  **B:** No, they thought about buying one, but for some reason, they are not moving forward with that decision.

-------





### (ii) Negative Sentences

To talk about something that someone **will not have** in the future, we use "will not have."


- *"Will not have"* (won't have) is used to indicate that the possession will not happen in the future.

```
Examples:

- I will not have enough time to finish the project by tomorrow.
- They will not have enough resources to complete the task on their own.
- She will not have the necessary documents for the meeting.
```


**Real-time scenario:**

*At a grocery store:* I *won't have* enough money to buy groceries this week because I spent too much last week.

*Futuristic scenario:* In the year 2050, people *won't have* physical wallets or cash. All transactions will be digital.




**Conversation Examples:**

>- **A:** Will you **have** the report ready by tomorrow?  
  **B:** No, I **won't have** enough time. Maybe the day after tomorrow.
  

  
- **A:** Will they **have** a new member joining the team?  
  **B:** No, they **won't have** anyone joining soon. The position is still open.

-------




**A quick look**

To express possession, we use different tenses based on the time frame:

1. **Present Tense:** 
   * **Positive:** "I have a car."
   * **Negative:** "I don't have a car."
2. **Past Tense:** 
   * **Positive:** "I had a bike."
   * **Negative:** "I didn't have a bike."
3. **Future Tense:** 
   * **Positive:** "I will have a house."
   * **Negative:** "I won't have a house."
   
   -------
   
   


###Reminders:

**Present:** "Have/has" or "do not have/does not have."
**Past:** "Had" or "did not have."
**Future:** "Will have" or "will not have."
