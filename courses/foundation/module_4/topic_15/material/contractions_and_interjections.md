# Contractions and Interjections

##Contractions
*A contraction is formed when two words are combined into a shorter word by omitting certain letters and replacing them with an apostrophe.*

####Example 1
```
"Do not" becomes "don't": The words "do" and "not" are combined to form the contraction "don't." 
To create this contraction, the letter "o" from "not" is removed, and an apostrophe is placed where the letter was omitted, resulting in "don't."
```
####Example 2
```
"I am" becomes "I'm": The words "I" and "am" are combined to form the contraction "I'm." 
In this case, the letter "a" from "am" is removed, and an apostrophe is used in its place, forming "I'm."
````




**Where do we use Contractions?**

- *Used to demonstrate the informal tone of speaking.*
- *Used in creating question tags.*



**Few Examples:**

- She is going to the store. → She's going to the store.
- I have a dog. → I've a dog.
- They are my best friends. → They're my best friends.
- They would like to join us. → They'd like to join us.





###Contraction Types


**Two types**

1. *Word + verb*

#####Examples

- I + am = I'm
- Let + us = Let's
- We + would = We'd

**Distinguishing Between 'Had' and 'Would' in Contractions:**

- **Contraction Use**: The contraction **'d** can represent either *"had"* or *"would"* (e.g., "she’d" could mean "she had" or "she would").

- **How to Tell the Difference**:
  - If **'d** is followed by a *past participle* (e.g., "she’d gone," "we’d seen"), it stands for *"had"* (used in the past perfect tense).
  - If **'d** is followed by a *base verb* (e.g., "she’d go," "we’d like"), it stands for *"would"* (often indicating future intentions or hypothetical situations).

- **Examples**:
  - *"She’d eaten before they arrived"* - **she had eaten before they arrived**.
  - *"She’d eat that if she could"* - **she would eat if she could**.
  
--------------------------------------------------------------



**Difference between some contractions and their corresponding non-contracted words**

- **Your** & **You're** (*Your* shows possession, *You're* means *You are*)
- **Whose** & **Who's** (*Whose* shows possession, *Who's* means *Who is*)
- **Its** & **It's** (*Its* shows possession, *It's* means *It is*)
- **There** & **They're** (*There* refers to a place, *They're* means *They are*)

## Interjections

*Interjections are words that are used to express emotions, feelings, and reactions.*

####How to Identify an interjection in a sentence?

- Words followed by an exclamation mark (!).
- (Interjections) are usually applied at the beginning of a sentence.


####Few Examples: 
```
- Oh no!
- Hurray!
- Yay!
- Ouch!
- Wow!```
