# Past Simple 3

##Simple Past: Past Identity or Status

###Definition

The simple past tense can be used to describe a **past identity, status, or condition** that existed before but is **no longer true in the present**. When talking about someone's past identity or how something was, we often use **"was"** or **"were"** to show this.

###Sentence Structure

###1. Positive Sentences

**Using “was”**

I/ He/ She/ That/ This/ any singular noun (except you) + **was** + information.

**Examples**

- I **was tired** after the long day.
- She **was a nurse** before she retired.
- He **was the manager** of the store last year.
- It **was a popular movie** when it was released.
- The dog **was sick** a few days ago.

**Using “were”**
 
You/ They/ We/ Those/ These/ any plural noun + **were** + information.

**Examples**

- We **were excited** about the trip last week.
- They **were teammates** in high school.
- The children **were quiet** during the movie.
- The books **were popular** a few years ago.
- You **were best friends** when you were younger.


**Everyday Conversation Examples**

- A: Why didn’t Mark join the meeting yesterday?
B: **He was busy** with another project. 
- A: Were your friends happy with the new restaurant?
B: Yes, **they were satisfied** with the food and service.
- A: You seem so confident now. Were you always like this?
B: No, **I was very shy** when I was younger.

###2. Negative Sentences
**Using “was not”**
I/ He/ She/ That/ This/ any singular noun (except you) + **was not** + information.

**Examples**

- I **was not the winner** of the competition.
- He **was not a member** of the club last year.
- That **was not the solution** to the problem.

**Using “were not”**

You/ They/ We/ Those/ These/ any plural noun + **were not** + information.

**Examples**

- You **were not the first person** to arrive. 
- We **were not the hosts** of the event.
- The books **were not the required materials** for the course.

**Points to Remember**
“Was” is used with I/ He/ She/ That/ This/ any singular noun (except you).
“Were” is used with You/ They/ We/ Those/ These/ any plural noun.

**Everyday Conversation Examples**

- A: I heard John was the keynote speaker at the conference.
B: No, **he was not the keynote speaker**. He just moderated one of the panels.
- A: Were the dogs the cause of the noise last night?
B: No, **they were not the cause of the noise**. It was actually coming from a nearby construction site.
- A: Were those sculptures part of the original exhibition?
B: No, **they were not part of the original exhibition**. They were added later for a special display.
