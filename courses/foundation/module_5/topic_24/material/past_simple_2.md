# Past Simple 2

#Simple Past Tense: A Habit or Usual Activity in the Past

##Definition

Simple past tense is used to talk about actions, habits, or situations that **were true or happened regularly in the past**, but no longer occur or are **no longer true in the present**. It helps express things that were part of someone’s routine or a past state that has now changed.

###Sentence Structure

###1. Positive Sentences

To describe past habits, routines or conditions that don’t happen now or have changed since, simple past tense sentences follow the structure,

```
I/ you/ we/ any plural/singular noun/ they/that/ this/those/these/ he/ she/ it + used to + verb (I form)
```
**Note:** The verb that follows "used to" stays in its original form (I form) because "used to" already indicates a past habit or situation.

**Examples**

- They **used to own** a small bookstore downtown.
- That **used to belong** to my grandfather before he passed it down to me.
- He **used to drive** to work before he started taking the train.
- I **used to code** in Java before switching to Python.
- It **used to be** a lively park, but now it's mostly deserted.

**Everyday Conversation Examples**

- A: Why are these old photos stored away?
B: Because **these used to hang** on the wall, but we took them down when we redecorated.

- A: Did your friends always hang out at the new café?
B: No, **they used to go** to the old diner down the street.

- A: It looks like you're more active now.
B: That's true. We **used to exercise** only once a week, but now it's almost daily.

###2. Negative Sentences

These sentences are used to express actions, habits, or states that **did not happen regularly or were not true in the past**. This structure helps indicate that something was not part of a routine or a common situation in the past.

The structure of these negative sentences are:

```
I/ you/ we/ any plural/ singular noun/ they/that/ this/those/these/ he/ she/ it + did not + use to + Verb ( I form)
```
**Example Sentences**

- I **did not use to like** vegetables when I was a child.
- She **did not use to play** sports in high school.
- They **did not use to travel** during the holidays.

**Everyday Conversation Examples**

- A: Have you always been a coffee drinker?
B: No, I **did not use to drink** coffee at all. I only started last year.
- A: Did your dog always behave so well?
B: No, he **did not use to listen** to commands very well. It took a lot of training.
- A: Your family seems to enjoy traveling a lot.
B: That's true now, but we **did not use to travel** much when I was a kid.
