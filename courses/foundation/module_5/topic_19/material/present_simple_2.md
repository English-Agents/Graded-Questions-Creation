# Present Simple: Factual Descriptions

In this section, we focus on factual descriptions using both positive and negative sentences. Let's break down the rules and look at some examples to help you understand how to use this tense correctly.

## Rules for Factual Descriptions

###Positive Sentences for Factual Descriptions
#### 1. For singular subjects:

- I + am + (fact information)
- He/ She/ It/ any singular noun (except you)/ that/ this + is + (fact information).



**Examples:** 

```
He is a teacher.
She is my sister.
I am a professor.
There is a book on the table.
   ``` 

#### 2. For plural subjects:

You/ we/ any plural noun/ there/ these/ those + are + (fact information).

**Examples:** 

```
They are cousins.
We are friends.
Those are my shoes.
There are five chairs in the room.
```





### Negative Sentences for Factual Descriptions

#### 1. Singular Subjects:
**Rule:** 

- He/ She/ It/ any singular noun (except you) /that /this + is not + (fact information).
- I + am  not  + (fact information).

 **Examples:** 
 
 ```
He is not a doctor.
She is not my cousin.
I am not an engineer.
```

#### 2. Plural Subjects:

**Rule:** 

  You/ we/ any plural noun/ there/ these/ those + are  not + (fact information)

 **Examples:** 
 
 ```
They are not friends.
We aren't musicians.
Those are not my books.
 ```   



###Examples:

Here are some sample conversations to illustrate how factual descriptions are used in daily life:

>**Conversation 1:**
- Person A: The bank opens at 9 a.m. every day.
- Person B: Yes, but it **isn't open** on Sundays.

>**Conversation 2:**
- **A:** What are these?
- **B:** These **are** my keys.

>**Conversation 3:**
- Person A: Did you bring the reports for the meeting?
- Person B: The reports **are not ready** for discussion yet.

>**Conversation 4:**
- **A:** Is that a new phone?
- **B:** No, it **is not**. It **is** an old model.

**Using "There Is" vs. "There Are" in Simple Terms**

#### 1. "There Is" – used for Singular and uncountable Nouns
Use "there is" when talking about one thing (singular) or uncountable nouns (things that cannot be counted individually).


### Examples:
- There is a book on the table. (Only one book)
- There is some water in the glass.




#### 2. "There Are" – used for Plural Nouns
Use **"there are"** when talking about **more than one** thing (plural).

### Example:
- There are two books on the table. (More than one book)


**Remember:**

- Use "there is" with singular and uncountable nouns.
- Use "there are" with plural nouns.
