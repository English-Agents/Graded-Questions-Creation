# Present Simple: Usual Activities

The *simple present tense* is commonly used to describe habitual or usual activities. It also states facts, general truths, and scheduled events.

## 1. Sentence Structure

### 1.1 Positive Sentences

**Rule:**  

- He/ She/ It/ any singular nouns (except you)/ this/ that + Verb (1st form) + **s/es**  
- I/ You/ We/ any plural nouns/ these/ those) + Verb (1st form)



**Examples:** 

- I **eat** breakfast at 7 a.m. every day.
- She **goes** to school by bus.
- They **play** football every weekend.




> **Note:** 

>For singular nouns & pronouns (he, she, it), add **s** or **es** to the verb.
> e.g. He **runs**, She **reads**.

> For verbs ending in **-sh, -ch, -ss, -x, -o**, add **es** to them, if the noun is singular.
e.g. She **watches** TV, He **fixes** the car.

---



### 1.2 Negative Sentences

**Rule:** 

- He/She/It, any singular noun/ this/ that+ *does not*+ Verb (V1)  
- I/You/We, any plural noun/ these/ those) +*do not*+ Verb (V1)




**Examples:** 

- I **do not** eat breakfast at 7 a.m.
- She **does not** go to school by bus.
- They **do not** play football every weekend.





> **Note:** Use **does not** for singular nouns & pronouns (he, she, it) and **do not** for plural nouns and pronouns.





###Real-Time Conversation Examples

>- **Person A:** Should I send this email now?  
  **Person B:** No rush! We usually **send** it at the end of the day.

>- **Person A:** Does she come to the office on weekends?  
  **Person B:** No, she doesn't. She **prefers** to work from home.






###Tips and Reminders

- For **singular subjects**,(except you) always add **s** or **es** to the verb in positive sentences.
- In **negative sentences**, use **do not (don't)** for subjects like *I, you, we, they* and **does not (doesn't)** for *he, she, it*.
- Always use the **first form of the verb** in negative sentences, regardless of the subject.

## Notes

- The **Present Simple** is not only for usual activities but also for general truths (e.g., *The sun rises in the east*) and scheduled events (e.g., *The train leaves at 6 p.m.*).
- Common time expressions used with the Present Simple include: *always*, *usually*, *often*, *sometimes*, *never*, *every day*, *on Sundays*, *in the morning*, etc.
