# Present Simple 3

#Present Simple: Current Status of Happenings

Present Simple tense sentences are used to describe **the current status or condition of something that is happening now**. 

They can be used for the following scenarios:

| **Scenario / Application**         | **Example**                                | **Purpose**                                                |
|------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------|
| Processes and procedures          | The machines are cleaned every morning.    | To describe regular steps where the agent is not needed    |
| Rules, regulations, or permissions| Mobile phones are not allowed inside.      | To state institutional or social rules                     |
| Formal statements or official language | The policy is reviewed annually.         | To make writing impersonal and objective                    |
| News headlines or factual reporting | The bridge is opened to the public.        | To emphasize the event/result                              |
| Instructions (impersonal)         | The form is submitted online.              | To give steps without mentioning the doer                   |
| When the doer is unknown or irrelevant | The window is broken.                    | Focus is on the result, not who did it                      |
| Scientific or universal truths (in passive) | Water is purified before it is bottled. | To explain natural or technical processes                  |
| Education / Academic contexts     | Essays are evaluated based on clarity.     | To state assessment criteria without naming evaluators      |

## 1. Sentence Structure

### 1.1 Positive Sentences

**Rule**

```
I + am + verb (III form) + rest of the sentence.
He/ She/ It/ any singular noun (except you) + is + verb (III form) + rest of the sentence.
You/ We/ They/ any plural noun + are + verb (III form) + rest of the sentence.
```

**Examples**

- The project **is approved** by the manager.
- We **are located** in the downtown area.
- I **am involved** in the community events.
- You **are expected** to attend the conference.
- He **is invited** to the meeting.

| Noun                              | Sentence Structure                               | Examples                                                         |
|------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------|
| I                                  | am + verb (III form) + rest of the sentence      | I am assigned a new task by my manager. 
 I am provided with all the necessary resources. |
| He/ She/ It/ any singular noun     | is + verb (III form) + rest of the sentence      | The report is completed and ready for review. 
 She is consulted before any major decision is made. |
| You/ We/ They/ any plural noun     | are + verb (III form) + rest of the sentence     | They are informed about the schedule. 
 The documents are signed by the manager. |


### 1.2 Negative Sentences

**Rule**

```
I + am + not + verb (III form) + rest of the sentence.
He/ She/ It/ any singular noun (except you) + is + not + verb (III form) + rest of the sentence.
You/ We/ They/ any plural noun + are + not + verb (III form) + rest of the sentence.
```

**Examples**

- He **is not included** in the meeting.
- She **is not assigned** to the task.
- You **are not accepted** into the club.
- I **am not chosen** for the team.
- They **are not permitted** to enter the building.

| Noun                              | Sentence Structure                               | Examples                                                         |
|------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------|
| I                                  | am + not + verb (III form) + rest of the sentence| I am not allowed to use my phone during the exam. 
 I am not monitored during the test. |
| He/ She/ It/ any singular noun     | is + not + verb (III form) + rest of the sentence| She is not qualified for the position. 
 The management is not notified about the changes. |
| You/ We/ They/ any plural noun     | are + not + verb (III form) + rest of the sentence| They are not evaluated until next week. 
 The documents are not processed yet. |
