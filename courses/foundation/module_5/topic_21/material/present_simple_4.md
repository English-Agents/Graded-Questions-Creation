#Present Simple: Command, Comments and Suggestions

The simple present tense is often used in everyday communication to give commands, make comments, and provide suggestions. 

### Commands

Commands are statements that **give direct instructions or orders** to someone. They are direct and forceful. 

- When giving commands, we use **the simplest form of the verb**, like "sit" or "run." 
- We **don’t use "you" in commands** because it’s clear that we’re talking to the person directly. 
- In commands, the verb is used directly.


Here are a few examples that will help you understand the concept.

**Examples** (Positive Sentences)

- **Close the door.**
Meaning:  When you say "Close the door," it’s a short way of telling someone to do something. It really means "You close the door," but we don’t say "you" because the person knows you are talking to them.

- **Open the book.**
Meaning: It really means "You open the book," but we don't need to say "you" because the person understands that you're talking to them.

- **Sit down.**
Meaning: It really means “You sit down,” but we don't need to say "you" because the person understands that you're talking to them.

**Examples** (Negative Sentences)

- Don’t miss the deadline.
- Don’t talk during the lecture.
- Don’t park here.
- Don’t touch the exhibits.

(**Note:** In negative sentences for commands, *we always use “don’t”* because it is addressed to “you”.)

**More Examples**

- Stand up.
- Turn off the light.
- Put away your phone.
- Attend the lecture on time.
- Don’t forget to lock the door.
- Don’t run in the hallway.
- Don’t touch the stove.
- Don’t forget your keys.

###Comments

Comments made using the simple present tense **make direct observations, or state facts about the person you're speaking to,** that are generally relevant in a given situation. Comments don’t give instructions like commands; instead, they describe something that is true or relevant at that point.

**Examples**

- You look tired. - Here, the speaker observes the listener's current state. 
- You speak very clearly. -This is an observation that may be made during or after a conversation, praising the person’s speaking ability.
- You have a great sense of humor. - This reflects the speaker’s observation based on their interaction with the listener.

###Suggestions

Suggestions are polite ways to **recommend actions or advice**.  Unlike commands, which are direct, suggestions offer guidance or advice in a softer, more considerate manner.

**Examples**

- You can take the bus if you don’t want to drive.
- He can call the customer service department for help.
- It can take time to learn a new skill.
- They can try different solutions to fix the issue.
- The team may need additional resources to complete the task.
- She may benefit from practicing her presentation more.
- He may want to rethink his decision.

##Sentence Structure: Commands, Comments and Suggestions

###Positive Sentences

| Noun                                      | Sentence Structure                                       | Example                                           |
|-------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------|
| You                                       | + verb (I form) + rest of the sentence.                  |- You work hard on your projects. 
 - You speak English very well.                      |
| (You is silent here)                      | + verb (I form) + rest of the sentence.                  | - Speak louder.

| I/ you/ he/ she/ it/ they/ all plural nouns/ all singular nouns | + can/ may + verb (I form) + rest of the sentence.       | - They may need to hire more staff to meet the deadline.

###Negative Sentences

| Noun                           | Sentence Structure                                          | Example                                           |
|---------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------|
