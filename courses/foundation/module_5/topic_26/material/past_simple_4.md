# Past Simple 4


##Simple Past: Information about the Status of a Past Activity

###Definition
We use sentences in the simple past tense to  focus on the status or outcome of a past activity. This type of sentence informs us about what happened to something or someone and gives an update on the condition or result of the action in the past.

###Sentence Structure

###1. Positive Sentences

**Using “was”**

```
I/ She/ He/ That/ This/ It/ any singular noun (except you) + was + verb (III form) + rest of the sentence.
```

**Examples**

- The car **was parked** in the garage.
- The book **was borrowed** from the library last week.
- She **was awarded** a scholarship for her achievements.
- He **was blamed** for the mistake. 
- I **was informed** about the changes yesterday.

**Using “were”**

```
You/ we/ any plural noun/those/these/ they + were + verb (III form) + rest of the sentence.
```

**Examples**

- The houses **were damaged** during the storm.
- The tickets **were sold out** within an hour.
- Those documents **were misplaced** during the office move.
- We **were instructed** to follow a different procedure for the project.
- They **were warned** about the potential risks before starting.

**Everyday Conversation Examples**

- A: Was the meeting rescheduled after the cancellation?
B: Yes, it **was postponed** to next Monday.
- A: Did the artwork win any awards?
B: Yes, it **was recognized** at the art show for its creativity.
- A: Were the issues addressed during the discussion?
B: Yes, they **were resolved** before the meeting ended.

###2. Negative Sentences

**Using “was not”**

```
I/ She/ He/ That/ This/ It/ any singular noun (except you) + was not + verb (III form) + rest of the sentence.
```

**Examples**

- I **was not chosen** for the team.
- She **was not informed** about the meeting.
- The project **was not completed** on time.
- That package **was not delivered** yesterday.
- The problem **was not resolved** before the deadline.

**Using “were not”**

```
You/ we/ any plural noun/those/these/ they + were not + verb (III form) + rest of the sentence.
```
**Examples**

- They **were not consulted** before the decision was made.
- You **were not considered** for the promotion.
- The reports **were not verified** before being published.
- Those items **were not inspected** for quality.
- We **were not included** in the final list of participants.

**Everyday Conversation Examples**

- A: I thought the documents were sent to the client.
B: No, they **were not dispatched** because there was a problem with the address.

- A: Was the cake prepared by the chef?
B: No, it **was not baked** in the kitchen; we ordered it from a bakery.

- A: I thought the problem was discussed at the meeting.
B: No, it **was not addressed** because there was not enough time.

```
Point to Remember

Do not use an adjective instead of the verb (III form). 
Always check whether the word after was/ were is an adjective or a verb.
For example: “We were exhausted after yesterday’s party.”
In this example, 
“exhausted” describes the condition of “we” and thus, it is not a verb, but an adjective.
```
