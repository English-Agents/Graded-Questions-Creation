#  What is Direct and Indirect Speech?

When we communicate, we often need to express what someone said. We can do this in two ways: by using the speaker’s exact words or by reporting the same meaning in our own words. These two methods are called **Direct Speech** and **Indirect Speech**.

### Direct Speech (Reported/Reporting Speech)

Direct speech is used when we write or speak the exact words said by a person. It is written inside quotation marks (“ ”). Direct speech helps the reader clearly understand what was spoken, and it is commonly used in dialogues in stories, dramas, and everyday conversation.

**Example:**

> Maya said, “I am busy now.”

### Indirect Speech (Reported Speech)

Indirect speech is used when we report what someone said without using their exact words. In indirect speech, quotation marks are not used. Instead, we focus on the meaning of the sentence. Indirect speech is commonly used in formal writing, summaries, news reports, and when we want to describe a conversation briefly.

**Example:**

> Maya said that she was busy then.

## Why is it important?

Direct and indirect speech is important because it helps us communicate clearly in both spoken and written English. Many times, we need to repeat the information that others have said. Instead of quoting the entire sentence every time, indirect speech allows us to report the message easily.

This skill is especially useful in storytelling, letter writing, essays, interviews, and conversations.

Students also learn direct and indirect speech because it strengthens grammar skills such as:
- tense changes  
- pronoun changes  
- question formation  

##  Where is it used?

Direct and indirect speech is used in many real-life situations.

### Direct speech is commonly used in:
- stories, novels, and plays (to show dialogues)  
- conversations (to repeat exact words)  

### Indirect speech is commonly used in:
- newspapers and interviews (to report what someone said)  
- formal writing (summaries, reports, emails)  
- narration in stories (to describe what characters said)  


# Core Rules: Direct → Indirect

## 1)Pronoun Changes (Very Important)

Pronouns usually change depending on who is speaking and who is listening. This is because the speaker and the listener may change when we report a speech.

| Type | Direct Speech | Indirect Speech |
|-----|--------------|----------------|
| First person (I / we) changes according to the speaker | He said, “I am ill.” | He said that he was ill. |
| Second person (you) changes according to the listener | She said to them, “You have done your work.” | She told them that they had done their work. |
| Third person (he / she / they) usually stays the same | He says, “She dances well.” | He says that she dances well. |

## 2) Tense Changes (Backshift Rule)

When the reporting verb is in the past (said, told, asked), the tense of the reported verb usually moves one step back.

### Tense Change Table

| Direct Speech Tense | Indirect Speech Tense | Example |
|-------------------|----------------------|---------|
| Present Simple | Past Simple | “I live here.” → He said that he lived there. |
| Present Continuous | Past Continuous | “I am reading.” → She said that she was reading. |
| Present Perfect | Past Perfect | “I have finished.” → He said that he had finished. |
| Past Simple | Past Perfect | “I bought a car.” → She said that she had bought a car. |
| Will | Would | “I will study tomorrow.” → He said that he would study the next day. |

**Important Note:**  
If the reporting verb is in the present or future (says, will say), the tense of the reported verb does not usually change.

**Example:**

> He says, “I am tired.” → He says that he is tired.

## 3) Time and Place Word Changes

Some words related to time and place change when we convert direct speech into indirect speech. This is done because the time and place of the original speech may be different from the time and place when we report it.

### Time and Place Change Table

| Direct Word | Indirect Word | Example |
|------------|--------------|---------|
| now | then | “I am busy now.” → She said that she was busy then. |
| today | that day | “I will go today.” → He said that he would go that day. |
| yesterday | the day before / the previous day | “I came yesterday.” → She said that she had come the day before. |
| tomorrow | the next day / the following day | “I will come tomorrow.” → He said that he would come the next day. |
| here | there | “I live here.” → He said that he lived there. |
| this | that | “I like this book.” → She said that she liked that book. |
| these | those | “These are mine.” → He said that those were his. |

# Statements, Questions, and Commands

## 4) Changing Statements into Indirect Speech

When we change a statement from direct speech to indirect speech, we usually use **“that.”**  
(The word *that* may be used or omitted.)

| Direct Speech | Indirect Speech |
|--------------|----------------|
| “I am happy.” | She said that she was happy. |
| “We are ready.” | They said that they were ready. |
| “He has a new phone.” | She said that he had a new phone. |

## 5) Changing Yes/No Questions into Indirect Speech

When a yes/no question is changed into indirect speech, we use **asked if** or **asked whether**.  
The question order changes into statement order.

| Direct Speech | Indirect Speech |
|--------------|----------------|
| “Are you coming?” | He asked if I was coming. |
| “Do you like tea?” | She asked whether I liked tea. |
| “Have you finished your work?” | He asked if I had finished my work. |

**Common Error:**
- ❌ He asked if was I coming.  
- ✅ He asked if I was coming.

## 6) Changing Wh- Questions into Indirect Speech

We keep the wh-word, but change the word order into statement order.

| Direct Speech | Indirect Speech |
|--------------|----------------|
| “Where do you live?” | He asked where I lived. |
| “What are you doing?” | She asked what I was doing. |
| “When will you return?” | He asked when I would return. |

## 7) Changing Requests and Commands into Indirect Speech

Requests and commands use **told / ordered / requested + to + verb**.  
Negative commands use **not to + verb**.

| Direct Speech | Indirect Speech |
|--------------|----------------|
| “Please sit down.” | He requested me to sit down. |
| “Close the door.” | She told him to close the door. |
| “Do not make noise.” | The teacher ordered the students not to make noise. |


# Important Exceptions

## Exception 1: Universal Truths and Scientific Facts

| Direct Speech | Indirect Speech |
|--------------|----------------|
| “The sun rises in the east.” | He said that the sun rises in the east. |
| “Water boils at 100°C.” | She said that water boils at 100°C. |
| “The earth moves round the sun.” | The teacher said that the earth moves round the sun. |

## Exception 2: Situations That Are Still True

| Direct Speech | Indirect Speech |
|--------------|----------------|
| “I live in Chennai.” | He said that he lives in Chennai. |
| “I work at a hospital.” | She said that she works at a hospital. |
| “My brother studies in college.” | He said that his brother studies in college. |

## Exception 3: Reporting Verb in the Present or Future

| Direct Speech | Indirect Speech |
|--------------|----------------|
| He says, “I am fine.” | He says that he is fine. |
| She says, “I finished the task.” | She says that she finished the task. |
| He will say, “I will help you.” | He will say that he will help me. |

## Exception 4: Past Perfect and Past Perfect Continuous Remain UNchanged

| Direct Speech | Indirect Speech |
|--------------|----------------|
| “I had already finished my homework.” | She said that she had already finished her homework. |
| “They had left before noon.” | He said that they had left before noon. |
| “I had been watching the movie.” | She said that she had been watching the movie. |

## Exception 5: Would, Should, Could, Might and Must Do Not Change

| Direct Speech | Indirect Speech |
|--------------|----------------|
| “I would help you.” | He said that he would help me. |
| “You should study.” | She said that I should study. |
| “It might rain.” | He said that it might rain. |

## Exception 6: Fixed Time Mentioned Clearly

| Direct Speech | Indirect Speech |
|--------------|----------------|
| “I will come on Monday.” | He said that he would come on Monday. |
| “The exam is on 15th March.” | She said that the exam is on 15th March. |
| “We are meeting at 6 PM.” | He said that they were meeting at 6 PM. |

## Exception 7: Reporting Immediately (Very Near Time)

| Direct Speech | Indirect Speech |
|--------------|----------------|
| “I am leaving now.” | He said that he was leaving now. |
| “She is coming today.” | He said that she was coming today. |
| “I will call you now.” | She said that she would call me now. |
