# Parallel Structures

## What is Parallel Structure?

When we speak or write in English, we often connect ideas in the same sentence. These ideas may appear in lists, paired expressions, or comparisons.  

**Parallel structure** means keeping the same grammatical pattern for ideas that are equally important.  

When the structure is balanced, the sentence sounds clear and natural.  
When the structure changes, the sentence becomes awkward or confusing.


## Basic Structure of Parallel Structures

| Structure | Description | Example Sentence |
|------------|-------------|------------------|
| Noun + noun + noun | A list of naming words | The job requires patience, accuracy, and confidence. |
| Verb + verb | Two actions joined together | The teacher explained the topic and answered the questions. |
| Adjective + adjective | Two describing words joined together | The instructions were clear and simple. |
| to + verb + to + verb | Two actions in the same “to” form | She hopes to improve and to succeed this year. |
| Clause + clause + clause | Balanced parts with their own verbs | He explained what happened, why it happened, and how it could be prevented. |


# Types of Parallel Structures

## 1) Parallel Structure in Lists

| Pattern | Common Use | Example Sentence |
|----------|------------|------------------|
| List of nouns | Listing ideas or qualities | The programme focuses on skills, confidence, and discipline. |
| List of verbs | Listing actions | Please read the instructions, check your answers, and submit the paper. |
| List of adjectives | Describing one thing in different ways | Her answer was clear, accurate, and detailed. |
| List using “to” + verb | Stating aims or plans | The aim is to reduce costs, to improve quality, and to increase efficiency. |


### Parallel Structure in Lists (Comparison)

| Pattern | Correct Sentence | Incorrect Sentence |
|----------|------------------|--------------------|
| Noun list | The report discusses causes, effects, and solutions. | The report discusses causes, effects, and how to solve the issue. |
| Verb list | The team collected evidence, checked sources, and presented findings. | The team collected evidence, checked sources, and the findings were presented. |
| Adjective list | The session was useful, engaging, and informative. | The session was useful, engaging, and it informed the students. |
| “to” form list | She plans to revise, to practise, and to attempt past papers. | She plans to revise, practising, and to attempt past papers. |


## 2) Parallel Structure with Conjunctions (and / or / but)

| Conjunction | Correct Sentence | Incorrect Sentence |
|--------------|------------------|--------------------|
| and | She reviewed the notes and completed the assignment. | She reviewed the notes and the assignment was completed. |
| or | You can email the form or hand it in at the office. | You can email the form or submission at the office. |
| but | The task was simple but time consuming. | The task was simple but it took a long time. |


## 3) Parallel Structure with Paired Conjunctions

### A) both…and

| Structure | Correct Sentence | Incorrect Sentence |
|------------|------------------|--------------------|
| nouns | The policy affects both students and teachers. | The policy affects both students and teaching staff are affected. |
| verbs | She both prepared the slides and delivered the presentation. | She both prepared the slides and the presentation was delivered. |
| adjectives | He is both confident and organised. | He is both confident and organises his work well. |


### B) not only…but also

| Structure | Correct Sentence | Incorrect Sentence |
|------------|------------------|--------------------|
| verbs | He not only solved the problem but also explained the solution. | He not only solved the problem but also an explanation of the solution. |
| adjectives | The workshop was not only useful but also interesting. | The workshop was not only useful but also it interested everyone. |
| nouns | The programme supports not only students but also teachers. | The programme supports not only students but also teachers are supported. |


### C) either…or / neither…nor

| Structure | Correct Sentence | Incorrect Sentence |
|------------|------------------|--------------------|
| either…or (verbs) | You can either join the meeting online or attend it in person. | You can either join the meeting online or an in person attendance. |
| either…or (nouns) | Bring either your passport or your identity card. | Bring either your passport or show your identity card. |
| neither…nor (verbs) | He neither apologised nor explained his behaviour. | He neither apologised nor an explanation was given. |
| neither…nor (nouns) | The box contained neither documents nor photographs. | The box contained neither documents nor was there any photograph. |


# Parallel Structures in Comparisons

| Comparison Type | Correct Sentence | Incorrect Sentence |
|------------------|------------------|--------------------|
| Preference | He prefers studying in the morning to studying at night. | He prefers studying in the morning to study at night. |
| Ability | She is better at solving problems than explaining solutions. | She is better at solving problems than to explain solutions. |
| Priority | The company focused more on improving quality than increasing speed. | The company focused more on improving quality than to increase speed. |


# Parallel Structures in the Passive Voice

| Active Voice | Passive Voice |
|--------------|--------------|
| The team designed the project and implemented the plan. | The project was designed and the plan was implemented. |
| The lecturer explained the theory and demonstrated the method. | The theory was explained and the method was demonstrated. |
| The company reviewed the policy and updated the guidelines. | The policy was reviewed and the guidelines were updated. |


# Exceptional Cases

## Mixed Patterns in One Sentence (Comparison)

| Situation | Clear Sentence | Unclear Sentence |
|------------|----------------|------------------|
| Noun list | The role requires discipline, teamwork, and confidence. | The role requires discipline, teamwork, and working confidently. |
| Descriptions | The talk was clear, engaging, and informative. | The talk was clear, engaging, and it informed everyone. |
| Two actions | She checked the details and sent the email. | She checked the details and the email. |


## Weak Balance with Paired Conjunctions (Comparison)

| Paired Conjunction | Clear Sentence | Unclear Sentence |
|---------------------|----------------|------------------|
| not only…but also | He not only improved his grades but also improved his attendance. | He not only improved his grades but also his attendance increased. |
| both…and | She both prepared the slides and delivered the presentation. | She both prepared the slides and the presentation was delivered. |


# Some Commonly Used Parallel Structures

## Everyday Usage

| Pattern | Example Sentence |
|----------|------------------|
| Routine list | She wakes up early, drinks tea, and starts work at eight. |
| Shopping list | I bought bread, milk, and vegetables from the shop. |
| Simple description | The film was funny and memorable. |
| Simple comparison | He enjoys cycling more than driving in the city. |


## Academic Usage

| Pattern | Example Sentence |
|----------|------------------|
| Essay plan | This essay discusses the causes of pollution, its effects on health, and possible solutions. |
| Research actions | The researchers collected data, analysed results, and reported findings. |
| Formal aims | The objective is to identify trends, to evaluate evidence, and to propose solutions. |
| Evaluation language | A strong argument is clear, logical, and well supported. |


## Professional Usage

| Pattern | Example Sentence |
|----------|------------------|
| Instructions | Please complete the form, attach the documents, and submit the application by Friday. |
| Policies | Employees must follow procedures, maintain confidentiality, and meet deadlines. |
| Reports | The company reduced costs, improved efficiency, and expanded services in 2025. |
| Meetings | We need to confirm the agenda, to assign roles, and to set deadlines today. |
