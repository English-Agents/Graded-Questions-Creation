# Real Time Session 2: Significance of Choosing Correct Words

# Classroom Activity: “Significance of Choosing Correct Words in Professional Communication”

## Overview

This activity helps students understand the importance of choosing precise, appropriate, and
impactful words in professional English communication. It trains learners to recognise how word
choice influences clarity, tone, persuasion, credibility, and audience response. Through
structured speaking tasks, students learn to replace vague, informal, or inappropriate language
with words that communicate ideas accurately and professionally. The activity enhances
speaking skills, vocabulary awareness, audience sensitivity, and communication effectiveness.

# Stepping Stones

## 1) Understanding the Power of Word Choice

Students learn that words influence:

- Meaning and interpretation.
- Professional image and credibility.
- Emotional impact on the audience.
- Clarity and effectiveness of communication.

**On stage move:** Always ask—“Does this word communicate exactly what I intend?”

## 2) Choosing Words Based on Context and Audience

Effective communication depends on selecting words suitable for:

- Formal or informal situations.
- Academic, professional, or social contexts.
- Audience knowledge and expectations.
- Tone required for the message.

**On stage move:** Replace casual expressions with professional alternatives when addressing
formal audiences.

## 3) Precision and Clarity in Communication

Correct word choice improves communication by:

- Avoiding ambiguity and confusion.
- Making ideas concise and accurate.
- Reducing unnecessary explanations.
- Increasing listener understanding.

**On stage move:** Use specific words instead of vague expressions like “things,” “stuff,” or
“something.”

## 4) Tone Management through Vocabulary

Words shape the tone of communication:

- Positive words encourage engagement.
- Diplomatic language reduces conflict.
- Confident wording increases persuasion.
- Respectful language builds professionalism.

**On stage move:** Focus on constructive and respectful vocabulary even during disagreement.

## 5) Reinforcing Message Impact through Effective Vocabulary

Strong communication becomes memorable when:

- Key words are repeated strategically.
- Powerful action verbs are used.
- Simple but impactful vocabulary is selected.
- Main ideas are expressed clearly and confidently.

**On stage move:** End important statements with strong and purposeful words.

# Divide the Class into Teams

## Speaking Teams

Students work in groups of 3–4.

Each group prepares a short presentation on a given topic (leadership, teamwork, education,
workplace communication, technology, etc.).

The focus is on demonstrating effective word choice and professional vocabulary during the
presentation.

## Audience Panel (Instructor + Students)

The panel listens and evaluates how effectively students use appropriate, precise, and
professional language.

They may ask questions to assess clarity, tone, and vocabulary usage.

# Role Allocation (Within Teams)

- **Opening Speaker** – Introduces the topic using clear and professional language.
- **Vocabulary Speaker** – Explains important word choices and their impact on communication.
- **Support Speaker** – Provides examples comparing weak and effective wording.
- **Closing Speaker** – Summarises the importance of choosing correct words.

# Rules

- Use professional and audience-appropriate vocabulary.
- Avoid slang, vague expressions, and repetitive wording.
- All members must participate.
- Focus on clarity and precision rather than difficult vocabulary.
- Avoid memorised long scripts; focus on natural delivery.

# Time Limit

- Preparation Time: 5–7 minutes.
- Presentation Time: 4–5 minutes per team.
- Q&A: 1–2 minutes.
- Feedback: 1 minute.

# Evaluation Criteria

Teams will be assessed on:

- Appropriate and precise word choice.
- Clarity and professionalism of language.
- Ability to maintain a suitable tone.
- Effectiveness of vocabulary in delivering ideas.
- Audience understanding and engagement.
- Confidence and fluency in speaking.

# Example Play

## Topic: Importance of Professional Language in the Workplace.

### Opening Speaker:

Good morning everyone. Today we are presenting on the significance of choosing correct
words in workplace communication. The words we choose affect professionalism, clarity, and
the way others respond to our ideas.

### Vocabulary Speaker:

The first key point is that precise words improve clarity. For example, saying “The project
requires revision” is clearer and more professional than saying “There are some issues with it.”

The second key point is that word choice influences tone. Respectful and diplomatic language
helps maintain positive workplace relationships and avoids unnecessary conflict.

The third key point is that strong vocabulary increases credibility. Professionals who
communicate clearly and confidently are often seen as more reliable and competent.

### Support Speaker:

For example, instead of saying “This idea is bad,” a professional speaker may say, “This
approach may need further improvement.” The second statement communicates disagreement
respectfully while maintaining professionalism.

### Closing Speaker:

To conclude, the most important points are: correct words improve clarity, shape professional
tone, and strengthen credibility. Careful word choice helps communication become more
effective, respectful, and impactful in professional environments.

# Conclusion

This activity strengthens a vital professional communication skill—choosing correct words
effectively. It enables students to communicate with accuracy, professionalism, and confidence,
ensuring that their ideas are clearly understood and positively received. By practising thoughtful
vocabulary selection and audience-focused communication, learners develop the ability to
speak with greater precision and impact in academic and professional contexts.
