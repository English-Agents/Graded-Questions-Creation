# Past Perfect Continuous

#Past Perfect Continuous
 
 
 
 ##Definition
 
 
 
 The Past Perfect Continuous tense is used to talk about 
 
 
 
 - an action that started in the past and continued for some time before another action or point in the past.
 
 - how long something had been happening.
 
 - an ongoing action before another past event.
 
 
 
 ```
 
 "Had been" and “-ing” form of the verb are the keywords here.
 
 ```
 
 **Examples**
 
 
 
 - I *had been waiting* for an hour before the bus finally arrived.
 
 - She *had been living* in that house for five years before she moved out.
 
 - They *had been playing* football when it suddenly started to rain.
 
 - He *had been working hard* all week, so he was very tired on Saturday.
 
 - We *had been driving for hours* when we stopped to take a break.
 
 
 
 ##Structure
 
 
 
 ###Positive Sentences**
 
 
 
 
 
 I/you/ we/ any plural noun/ those/ these/he/ she/ it/ any singular noun/this/ that + **had been + verb (-ing form)**
 
 
 
 **Examples**
 
 
 
 - She *had been practicing* the piano for weeks before the concert.
 
 - It *had been raining* continuously for two days before the sun finally came out.
 
 - They *had been playing* football for an hour when it started to rain.
 
 - I *had been exercising* regularly before I got injured.
 
 - You *had been waiting* for a response when the email finally arrived.
 

 ---
 
 
 ###Negative Sentences
 
 
 I/you/ we/ any plural noun/ those/ these/he/ she/ it/ any singular noun/this/ that + **had not been + verb (-ing form)**
 
 
 
 **Examples**
 
 
 
 - We *had not been expecting* such a large crowd before the event started.
 
 - I *had not been sleeping* properly before I decided to change my routine.
 
 - You *had not been paying* attention when the teacher explained the topic.
 
 - He *had not been studying* much before he failed the test.
 
 - She *had not been feeling* well before she visited the doctor.
