# Past Perfect

#Past Perfect Tense

##Definition

The past perfect tense is used to talk about **something that happened before another action in the past**. It helps us show the order of two past actions. **”Had”** is the keyword here.

**Examples**

- She **had finished** her homework **before dinner**.
 
 Here, first action : “She” finished her homework.
 
 Second action: “She” ate dinner.
 
- They **had left** the house when **I arrived**.
 
 Here, first action: They left the house.
 
 Second action: The speaker arrived.
 
- She **was tired** because she **had worked** all day.
 
 First action: She worked all day.
 
 Second action : She got tired.
 
- They **had visited** the museum before **they went** to the park.
 
 First action: They visited the museum.
 
 Second action: They went to the park.


- I **went home** after the **meeting had ended**.
 
 First action: The meeting ended.
 
 Second action: I went home.
 
 (**Note:** When we are talking about two past actions, we use past perfect (**had**) for the action which happened first (or earlier).)
 
 
 
 ##Structure
###1.Using “Had”
 
 
 
 **Positive Sentences**
 
 
 
 I/you/ we/ any plural noun/ those/ these/he/ she/ it/ any singular noun/this/ that + **had + verb (III form)**
 
 
 
 **Examples**
 
 
 
 ```
 John had studied for the exam before the class started.
 
 The children had played outside before it started raining.
 
 I had already left when you called me.
 
 You had completed your work before the meeting started.
 
 She had finished reading the book by the time I called her.
 
 We had already eaten when they arrived at the restaurant.
 
 ```
 
 
 
 **Negative Sentences**
 
 
 
 I/you/ we/ any plural noun/ those/ these/he/ she/ it/ any singular noun/this/ that + **had not + verb (III form)**
 
 
 
 **Examples**
 
 
 
 ```
 She had not finished her homework before she went to bed.
 
 They had not received the package when I called them.
 
 He had not heard about the meeting until yesterday.
 
 I had not tried that restaurant before last weekend.
 
 You had not called me back when I sent the reminder.
 
 ```
 
 (**Note:** In past perfect tense, “had” does not change its form depending on the subject.)
 
 
 
 
 
 ###2.Using “Had Been”
 
 
 
 We use “had been” in past perfect tense when we want to **emphasize the action or the receiver of an action**, and we also want to convey that the action was completed before another past event.
 
 
 
 **Positive Sentences**
 
 
 
 I/you/ we/ any plural noun/ those/ these/he/ she/ it/ any singular noun/this/ that + **had been + verb (III form)**
 
 
 
 **Examples**
 
 
 
 ```

 The emails had been sent by the time the office closed.
 
 The book had been read by all the students before the discussion.
 
 The dishes had been washed before they started cooking dinner.
 
 The tickets had been sold by the time we arrived at the event.
 
 The application had been processed before the deadline passed.
 
 ```
 
 **Negative Sentences**
 
 
 
 I/you/ we/ any plural noun/ those/ these/he/ she/ it/ any singular noun/this/ that + **had not been + verb (III form)**
 
 
 
 **Examples**
 
 
 
 ```

 The house had not been cleaned before the guests arrived.
 
 The documents had not been signed when the manager left.
 
 The cake had not been baked properly, so it tasted raw.
 
 The homework had not been submitted before the deadline passed.
 
 The room had not been decorated when the party started.
 
 ```



 #Past Perfect: Scenario Differentiation

The past perfect tense is often confused with other tenses. Here is a guide to differentiate past perfect tense with some of the other tenses:

1. Past Perfect (Had + V3) and Past Possession

2. Past Perfect (Had + V3) and Past Simple (V2)

3. Past Perfect (Had + V3) and Present Perfect ( Has/ Have + V3)

##1. Past Perfect (Had + V3) and Past Possession

| **Aspect** | **Past Perfect (had + V3)** | **Past Possession (had + noun/ noun and adjective)** |
|--------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------|
| **Definition** | Describes an action completed before another past event. | Describes ownership or possession of something in the past. |
| **Structure** | Subject + had + verb (III form) | Subject + had + noun/ combination of adjective and noun |
| **Example 1** | She had finished the report before the meeting began. | She had a beautiful car when she was younger. |
| | The action of finishing the report happened before the meeting started. | She owned a beautiful car in the past. |
| **Example 2** | They had eaten dinner before the guests arrived. | They had a beautiful house when they lived in the city. |
| | The action of eating dinner happened before the guests arrived. | "Had" shows they owned a beautiful house in the past. |
| **Example 3** | He had left the office by the time I called. | He had a meeting at 4 PM. |
| | The action of leaving the office was completed before I called. | "Had" indicates he had a scheduled meeting. |

##2. Past Perfect (Had + V3) and Past Simple (V2)

| **Aspect** | **Past Perfect (had + V3)** | **Simple Past (V2)** |
|--------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------|
| **Definition** | Describes an action that was completed before another past action or time. | Describes an action that happened at a specific time in the past. |
| **Structure** | Subject + had + verb (III form). | Subject + past tense verb (V2). |
| **Example 1** | She had left the office before the manager arrived. | She left the office at 5 PM. |
| | "Had left" happened before the manager's arrival (2 past actions). | "Left" happened at a specific time (5 PM). |
| **Example 2** | They had finished dinner when we got there. | They finished dinner an hour ago. |
| | "Had finished" shows dinner was completed before we arrived. | "Finished" refers to a specific past moment. |
| **Example 3** | He had already seen the movie when his friend invited him. | He saw the movie last weekend. |
| | "Had seen" emphasizes the movie was seen before the invitation. | "Saw" happened at a specific time (last weekend).|
| **Example 4** | By the time we reached, the train had departed. | The train departed at 6:30 PM. |
| | "Had departed" happened before we reached (2 past actions). | "Departed" happened at a definite time (6:30 PM).|
| **Example 5** | She had studied French before moving to Paris. | She studied French last year. |
| | "Had studied" shows studying French happened before moving to Paris. | "Studied" happened at a specific point (last year). |

##3. Past Perfect (Had + V3) and Present Perfect ( Has/ Have + V3)
| **Aspect** | **Past Perfect (had + V3)** | **Present Perfect (has/have + V3)** |
|--------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------|
| **Definition** | Describes an action that was completed before another action or time in the past. | Describes an action that happened at an unspecified time before now and is relevant to the present. |
| **Structure** | Subject + had + verb (III form). | Subject + has/have + verb (III form). |
| **Focus** | Focuses on the earlier action of two past events. | Focuses on the result of an action relevant to the present moment. |
| **Example 1** | She had left before the meeting started. | She has just left the office. |
| | "Had left" happened before another past action (meeting). | "Has left" refers to an action completed recently and is relevant now. |
| **Example 2** | They had finished dinner before the guests arrived. | They have already finished dinner. |
| | "Had finished" shows dinner was completed before guests arrived. | "Have finished" emphasizes that the action is complete now. |
| **Example 3** | He had visited Paris before he moved to London. | He has visited Paris several times. |
| | "Had visited" refers to something completed before another past action. | "Has visited" shows the experience is relevant to the present. |
| **Example 4** | By the time I called, she had gone to bed. | She has gone to bed, so you can’t talk to her. |
| | "Had gone" indicates the action happened before I called (past). | "Has gone" indicates the action is complete and relevant now. |
| **Example 5** | We had seen that movie before they recommended it. | We have seen that movie before. |
| | "Had seen" emphasizes the action was completed before the recommendation. | "Have seen" indicates we have experienced it at some time before now. |
