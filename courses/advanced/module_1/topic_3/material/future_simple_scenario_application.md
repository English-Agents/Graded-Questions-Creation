#Future Simple: Defining a Future State
##Defining Future Status
###Definition
The simple future tense is used for informing about the status of an action in the future. It is used to talk about **what will happen to something in the future**, especially when we focus on the result or the condition of an action. 

In such sentences, we do not focus on who is doing the action but rather on what will happen or what the condition of something will be in the future. **”Will be”** is the keyword here.

###Sentence Structure
####1. Positive Sentences

I/ you/ we/ any plural noun/singular noun/ they/that/ this/those/these/ he/ she/ it + **will + be + verb (III form)** + rest of the sentence.

**Examples:**

- The house *will be painted* by next week.
- The documents *will be sent* by the courier tomorrow.
- The report *will be finished* before the meeting.
- The package *will be delivered* by Monday.
- The invitations *will be printed* by the end of the day.

####2. Negative Sentences
I/ you/ we/ any plural noun/singular noun/ they/that/ this/those/these/ he/ she/ it + **will + not + be + verb (III form)** + rest of the sentence.
**Examples:**
- The documents *will not be sent* by the courier tomorrow.
- The house *will not be painted* by next week.
- The report *will not be finished* before the meeting.
- The package *will not be delivered* by Monday.
- The invitations *will not be printed* by the end of the day.

------

##Defining Future State
###Definition
Future simple tense is used when you want to describe **how someone or something will feel, behave, or appear in the future**. You are not talking about actions being done but rather the **condition or state of something or someone at a future time**.

###Sentence Structure
####1. Positive Sentences
I/ you/ we/ any plural noun/singular noun/ they/that/ this/those/these/ he/ she/ it + **will + be + adjective** + rest of the sentence.
**Examples**
- The weather *will be sunny* tomorrow.
- She *will be tired* after her long journey.
- The restaurant *will be busy* on Saturday night.
- We *will be ready* for the presentation by tomorrow.
- The cake *will be delicious*.

 ####2. Negative Sentences
 I/ you/ we/ any plural noun/singular noun/ they/that/ this/those/these/ he/ she/ it + **will + not + be + adjective** + rest of the sentence.
 **Examples**
 - The weather *will not be sunny* tomorrow.
 - She *will not be tired* after her long journey.
 - The restaurant *will not be busy* on Saturday night.
 - We *will not be ready* for the presentation by tomorrow.
 - The cake *will not be delicious*.

##Future Possession

Possession in Future Tense talks about something a person will have or possess in the future.

| | Noun | Sentence Structure | Examples |
|---------------|------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Positive | I/ you/ we/ any plural noun/singular noun/they/that/this/those/these/he/she/it | + will + have + noun/ combination of adjective and noun | I will have enough time to finish the project tomorrow. |
| | | | You will have the chance to meet her at the event. |
| | | | The students will have a break after the exams. |
| Negative | I/ you/ we/ any plural noun/singular noun/they/that/this/those/these/he/she/it | + will not + have + noun/ combination of adjective and noun | I will not have any money left if I buy this gadget. |
| | | | You will not have enough time to complete the assignment today. |

**Difference between “Will + Verb (I form)” and “Will be + Verb (III form)”**

| Structure | Usage | Example | Explanation |
|----------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Will + Verb (I form) | This form is used when you are talking about something that someone will do in the future. The person or thing doing the action is clear and stated in the sentence. | She will bake a cake. | Here, "will bake" tells us about an action in the future, and "she" is the person who is going to do the baking. |
| Will be + Verb (III form)| This form is used when you are talking about what will happen to something or someone in the future, without focusing on who will do the action. The person or thing doing the action is not mentioned or is less important. | The cake will be baked. | Here, "will be baked" tells us that the cake will be made in the future, but we don’t focus on who will bake it. The important part is that the cake will end up being baked. |
