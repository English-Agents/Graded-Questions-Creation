# Scenario Application: Simple and Continuous Tenses

#Simple Tenses
Simple tenses are the basic forms of verb tenses that indicate **when an action occurs** without specifying its duration or continuity. There are three simple tenses: Simple Present, Simple Past, and Simple Future.

```
Simple Present: Describes habitual actions, general truths, or facts.

Simple Past: Describes actions that happened and were completed in the past.

Simple Future: Describes actions that will happen in the future.
```

| Tense | Subject | Sentence Structure | Example | Negative Form | Example |

| | He/ She/ I/ You/ They/ any noun | Subject + has/have + object | They have a car. | Subject + do/does not + have + object | They do not have a car. |

#Continuous Tenses

Continuous tenses (also known as progressive tenses) describe actions that are **ongoing, in progress, or continuing** at a specific time. They emphasize the **duration** or progression of an action rather than its completion.

```
Present Continuous Tense: Describes an action that is happening right now or is ongoing around the present time.
Past Continuous Tense: Describes an action that was ongoing at a specific time in the past.
Future Continuous Tense: Describes an action that will be ongoing at a specific time in the future.

```

| Tense | Subject | Sentence Structure | Example | Negative Form | Example |
|----------------------|-----------------------------------|-------------------------------|-------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------|
| Present Continuous | I | I + am + V1-ing | I am walking to school. | I + am not + V1-ing | I am not walking to school. |
| | He/She/It/any singular noun (except you) | Subject + is + V1-ing | He is eating lunch. | Subject + is not + V1-ing | He is not eating lunch. |
| | You/We/They/any plural noun | Subject + are + V1-ing | They are studying English. | Subject + are not + V1-ing | They are not studying English. |
| Past Continuous | I/ He/She/It/any singular noun (except you) | Subject + was + V1-ing | She was cooking dinner. | Subject + was not + V1-ing | She was not cooking dinner. |
| | You/We/They/any plural noun | Subject + were + V1-ing | We were playing soccer. | Subject + were not + V1-ing | We were not playing soccer. |
| Future Continuous | I/He/She/It/You/We/They/any noun | Subject + will be + V1-ing | They will be traveling tomorrow. | Subject + will not be + V1-ing | They will not be traveling tomorrow. |


##Different Scenarios 
###1. Who does the action?
Look at the following examples:
**A)**
- He **informed** me about the upcoming exam. (Structure: He + V2). 
- He **was informed** about the upcoming exam. (Structure: He + was + V3)

**Explanation:**

**He informed me about the upcoming exam:** In this sentence, “he” is the one *doing the action*. It means “he” told “me” about the upcoming exam. “He” is the person who gave the information.

**He was informed about the upcoming exam:** In this sentence, something different is happening. “He” is not the one giving information. Instead, “he” is the one *receiving information* from someone else. It means someone else (about whom we have no information) told “he” about the exam. “He” did not do the action; someone did it to “he”.

**B)**
- She **gives** gifts to her friends. (Structure: She + V1(s))
- She **is given** gifts by her friends. (Structure: She + is + V3)

**Explanation:**

**She gives gifts to her friends:** It means that *she (the subject) is the one performing the action of giving gifts*, and her friends are the recipients of those gifts.

**She is given gifts by her friends:** It means that she (the subject) is the *recipient of the gifts*, and her friends are the ones performing the action of giving. The focus shifts to *her as the one receiving the gifts* rather than giving them.

**C)**
- They **will help** me with my homework.
- They **will be helped** with their homework.

**Explanation:**
 
**They will help me with my homework:** In this sentence, “they” will be giving help in the future. It means “they” plan or are expected to help “me” with my homework later. They will *take the action of helping someone*.

**They will be helped with their homework:** In this sentence, “they” will receive help in the future, but “they” won’t be the one providing it. It means *someone else will help “they”* with their homework later. Someone else (about whom the sentence does not mention) will be doing the action of helping “they”.

 | Tense | Structure | Example | Explanation | Present/Past/Future Difference |
 |-----------------|-------------------------|----------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------|
 | Present | is/am/are + V3 | She is taught French. | This means French lessons are being given to her by someone else. The action is happening to her now. | Describes a situation where something is being done to her in the present. |
 | | V1/V5 | She teaches French. | In this sentence, she is the one giving French lessons. She is doing the action herself. | Focuses on what she is doing now, showing her as the one performing the action. |
 | Past | was/were + V3 | She was taught French. | This indicates that French lessons were given to her by someone else in the past. The action happened to her. | Describes something that happened to her before, showing a past event. |
 | | V2 | She taught French. | Here, she was the one giving French lessons in the past. She did the action herself. | Shows what she did earlier, focusing on her as the person who completed the action. |
 | Future | will be + V3 | She will be taught French. | This suggests that French lessons will be given to her by someone else in the future. The action will happen to her. | Indicates something that will happen to her later, explaining a future event. |
 | | will + V1 | She will teach French. | In this case, she will be the one to give French lessons in the future. She plans to do the action herself. | Focuses on what she will do later, showing her as the one who will perform the action. |
 
 **More Examples**
 
 - They **showed** the artwork to the visitors. (They presented the artwork.)
 They **were shown** the artwork by the artist. (Someone presented the artwork to them.)
 
 - Mark **called** his manager to discuss the project. (He was the one who made the call.) 
 Mark **was called** by his manager to discuss the project. (His manager contacted him.)
 
 - She **will feed** her cat when she returns home. (She plans to give food to the cat.) 
 She **will be fed** breakfast by her mother. (Her mother will give her breakfast.)
 
 - He **drove** his brother to school yesterday. (He was the one who took him to school.)
 He **was driven** to work by his colleague yesterday. (His colleague took him to work.)
 
 - The turtle **moves** slowly across the sand. (The turtle is the one moving.)
 The turtle **is moved** to a safer area by the volunteers. (The volunteers are the ones moving the turtle.)
 
 - The phone **will charge** quickly if plugged in. (The phone is expected to charge.)
 The phone **will be charged** by the user overnight. (The user will charge the phone.)

 ###Different Sentence Structures
 
 | Tense | Sentence Structure | Example | Explanation |
 |-----------------|------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------|
 | **Present** | Subject + V1/V5 | The artist paints a picture. | The artist regularly paints a picture. |
 | | Subject + is/am/are + fact/opinion | The artist is talented. | This describes the artist's quality, telling us something about who they are. |
 | | Subject + is/am/are + V3 | The picture is painted by the artist. | The picture is being created by the artist, focusing on what happens to the picture. |
 | | Subject + is/am/are + V1-ing | The artist is painting a picture. | The artist is currently in the process of creating a picture. |
 | **Past** | Subject + V2 | The artist painted a picture. | The artist created a picture at a specific time in the past. |
 | | Subject + was/were + fact/opinion | The artist was famous. | This tells us about the artist’s status or quality in the past. |
 | | Subject + was/were + V3 | The picture was painted by the artist.| The picture was created by the artist at some point in the past. |
 | | Subject + was/were + V1-ing | The artist was painting a picture. | The artist was in the middle of creating a picture at that time. |
 | **Future** | Subject + will + V1 | The artist will paint a picture. | The artist plans to create a picture at some point in the future. |
 | | Subject + will be + fact/opinion | The artist will be well-known. | This predicts something about the artist's future status or quality. |
 | | Subject + will be + V3 | The picture will be painted by the artist. | The picture will be created by the artist at a later time. |
 | | Subject + will be + V1-ing | The artist will be painting a picture.| The artist will be in the process of creating a picture at a future time. |
 
 **More Examples**
 
 - Aravind **writes** books. (In this sentence, Aravind is actively creating or producing books regularly)<br>
 - Aravind **is writing** a book. (The focus is on what Aravind is doing right now or on the ongoing action.)
 - Aravind **is** a writer. (This tells us something about Aravind, stating a fact or opinion about him.)<br>
 - This book **is written** by Aravind. (In this case, the book is being created by Aravind. The action of writing is happening to the book, focusing on the outcome of Aravind’s work.)
 
 -------------------------------------------------------------------
 
 - The mechanic **fixed** the bike yesterday. (The mechanic made the bike work properly again at a point in the past.)
 - The mechanic **was fixing** the bike when it started to rain. (The mechanic was in the middle of making the bike work when the rain began.)
 - The mechanic **was** knowledgeable. (This tells us about the mechanic’s ability and understanding in the past.)
 - The bike **was fixed** by the mechanic. (The bike needed repair, and it was the mechanic who made it work properly. The focus is on the result of the action and not the doer of the action.)
 
 -------------------------------------------------------------------
 
 - The chef **will bake** a cake for the event. (The chef plans to make a cake in the future.)
 - The chef **will be baking** a cake tomorrow morning. (The chef will be in the process of making a cake at that time.)
 - The chef **will be busy** tomorrow. (This indicates that the chef will have a lot of work or tasks to do.)
 - The cake **will be baked** by the chef. (The cake will be made, and there is less importance given to the doer of the action.)
