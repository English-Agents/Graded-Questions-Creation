#Continuous Tenses 

Continuous tenses describe actions that are **ongoing or in progress** at a certain time. They show that something is happening around a specific moment or during a period of time. The sentences in continuous tenses use **“-ing form of the verb”**. 

**Examples**

- He was **cooking** dinner when the phone rang.
 
- She is **studying** for her exams.
 
- They will be **traveling** to Japan next month.

##Present Continuous Tense

###Definition

The present continuous tense describes actions that are **happening right now or around this moment**. It indicates that an action is currently in progress but has not yet finished.

###Structure

####1. Positive Sentences

Using **”am”**

I + **am + verb (-ing form)** + rest of the sentence.

**Examples**

- I **am reading** a book.
 
- I **am cooking** dinner.
 
- I **am writing** an email.


Using **”is”**

He / She / It / Singular nouns (except you)/ This/ That + **is + verb (-ing form)** + rest of the sentence.

**Examples**

- She **is watching** a movie.
 
- He **is fixing** his bike.
 
- The cat **is chasing** a ball.

Using **”are”**

You/ We / Plural nouns / They/ These/ Those + **are + verb (-ing form)** + rest of the sentence.

**Examples**

- You **are playing** soccer.
 
- We **are studying** for the exam.

- The children **are drawing** pictures.

####2. Negative Sentences

Using **”am not”**

I + **am not + verb (-ing form)** + rest of the sentence.

**Examples**

- I **am not watching** TV.

- I **am not eating** lunch.
 
- I **am not cleaning** the room.


Using **”is not”**

He / She / It / Singular nouns (except you)/ This/ That + **is not + verb (-ing form)** + rest of the sentence.

**Examples**

- He **is not reading** the newspaper.

- She **is not preparing** dinner.

- The dog **is not barking**.

Using **”are not”**

You/ We / Plural nouns / They/ These/ Those + **are not + verb (-ing form)** + rest of the sentence.

**Examples**

- They **are not playing** outside.
 
- We **are not working** on the project.
 
- The students **are not studying** in the library.

##Past Continuous

###Definition

The past continuous tense describes actions that were **happening at a specific time in the past**. These actions were ongoing in the past but may have been interrupted or stopped later.

###Structure

####1. Positive Sentences

Using **”was”**

I/ He / She / It / Singular nouns (except you)/ This/ That + **was + verb (-ing form)** + rest of the sentence.

**Examples**

- I **was reading** a book when the lights went out.
 
- She **was cooking** dinner at 7 PM.
 
- Rahul **was cleaning** the house all morning.


Using **”were”**

You/ We / Plural nouns / They/ These/ Those + **were + verb (-ing form)** + rest of the sentence.

**Examples**

- You **were working** late last night.
 
- They **were playing** soccer when it started to rain.
 
- The students **were discussing** their project after class.


####2. Negative Sentences

Using **”was not”**

I/ He / She / It / Singular nouns (except you)/ This/ That + **was not** + verb (-ing form) + rest of the sentence.

**Examples**

- I **was not jogging** in the park yesterday morning.
 
- She **was not watering** the plants in the garden yesterday.
 
- The dog **was not barking** at strangers last night.

Using **”were not”**

You/ We / Plural nouns / They/ These/ Those + **were not + verb (-ing form)** + rest of the sentence.

**Examples**

- You **were not studying** for the test at 9 PM.
 
- They **were not rehearsing** for the play last evening.

- The children **were not swimming** in the pool when the rain started.


##Future Continuous

###Definition
 
The future continuous tense describes actions that will be **happening at a specific time or during a period in the future**. It shows that something will be ongoing later. **”Will be”** is the keyword here.
 
###Structure

####1. Positive Sentences

I/ He/ She/ You/ We/ They/ These/ Those/ any plural or singular noun + **will be + verb (-ing form)** + rest of the sentence.
 
**Examples**
 
- He **will be working** on his car this weekend.
 
- They **will be celebrating** their victory tonight.
 
- I **will be traveling** to Japan next week.
 
- You **will be attending** the workshop next Friday.

####2. Negative Sentences

I/ He/ She/ You/ We/ They/ These/ Those/ any plural or singular noun + **will not be + verb (-ing form)** + rest of the sentence.


**Examples**
 
 
 
 - He **will not be exercising** at the gym this afternoon.
 
 - You **will not be watching** the movie with us tomorrow.
 
 - The workers **will not be repairing** the building next week.
 
 - I **will not be traveling** to the city next weekend.
 
**Conversation Examples**

- Person A: "I **was reading** a book when you called me last night."<br>Person B: "Oh, I didn’t know. I **wasn’t expecting** you to be busy."
 
- Person A: "I **am preparing** for the presentation right now."<br>Person B: "I thought you **were taking** a break. I didn’t realize you **were still working**."
 
- Person A: "I **will be traveling** to Paris next week for work."<br>Person B: "Really? I **will not be joining** you this time. I **will be staying** here to finish a project."
 
- Person A: "I **wasn’t studying** yesterday evening when you called; I **was just relaxing**."<br>Person B: "Well, I hope **you will be studying** for the exam next week. We have a lot to cover."
 
- Person A: "The kids **are playing** in the park right now."<br>Person B: "That’s nice. They **will be enjoying** their time before the weather changes tomorrow."
