#Scenario Application: Present Perfect and Present Perfect Continuous

##1. Present Perfect Tense: Application Scenarios

Present perfect tense follows two sentence structures:

1. Subject + **has/ have + Verb (III form)**

2. Subject + **has/ have + been + subject complement**

It is used to represent

- an action that just happened in the recent past and still has its effect in the present. 

- to indicate a state, condition, or situation that started in the past and continues into the present. 

- to describe someone's identity, role, or characteristics over a period.


###1.1 Recent Completed Action or Experience

| **Subject** | **Positive/Negative** | **Structure** | **Example** |
|----------------------|-----------------------|---------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------|
| He/ She/ It/ any singular noun | Positive | Has + V3 | He has finished his work. |
| | Negative | Has + not (hasn't) + V3 | He has not (hasn't) finished his work. |
| I/ You/ They/ We/ those/ these/ any plural noun | Positive | Have + V3 | They have traveled to Japan. |
| | Negative | Have + not (haven't) + V3 | They have not (haven't) traveled to Japan. |

###1.2 Indicate a Sate or Condition
| **Subject** | **Positive/Negative** | **Structure** | **Example** |
|----------------------|-----------------------|---------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------|
| He/ She/ It/ any singular noun | Positive | Has + been + Subject Complement | He has been tired after the workout. |
| | Negative | Has + not (hasn't) + been + Subject Complement | He has not (hasn't) been tired after the workout. |
| I/ You/ They/ We/ those/ these/ any plural noun | Positive | Have + been + Subject Complement | They have been excited about the news. |
| | Negative | Have + not (haven't) + been + Subject Complement | They have not (haven't) been excited about the news. |


###1.3 Describe Identity or Role

| **Subject** | **Positive/Negative** | **Structure** | **Example** |
|----------------------|-----------------------|---------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------|
| He/ She/ It/ any singular noun | Positive | Has + been + Subject Complement | He has been a doctor for 10 years. |
| | Negative | Has + not (hasn't) + been + Subject Complement | He has not (hasn't) been a doctor for long. |
| I/ You/ They/ We/ those/ these/ any plural noun | Positive | Have + been + Subject Complement | They have been teachers for over a decade. |
| | Negative | Have + not (haven't) + been + Subject Complement | They have not been members of the club for years. |

##2. Present Perfect Continuous

Present Perfect Continuous is used to indicate an action that started in the recent past and is still continuing in the present. It uses the **“-ing” form of the verb**.

| Subject | Positive/Negative | Structure | Examples |
|------------------|-------------------|----------------------------------|----------------------------------------------|
| He/She/It/That/This/ any singular noun | Positive | has been + verb(-ing) | She has been reading for an hour. |
| | Negative | has not been + verb(-ing) | He has not been working today. |
| I/ You/ They/ We/ Those/ These/ any plural noun | Positive | have been + verb(-ing) | We have been traveling all week. |
| | Negative | have not been + verb(-ing) | They have not been practicing enough. |
