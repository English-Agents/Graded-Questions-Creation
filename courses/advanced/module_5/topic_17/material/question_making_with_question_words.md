# Question Making with Question Words

#Question Making

There are two ways in which you can create questions.

1. With Question Words
2. With Question Verbs

** What are Question Words?**

Question words are used to ask questions and they help us get specific information about something. A list of question words is given below.

| **Question Word** | **Use** | **Example** |
|--------------------|--------------------------------------|---------------------------------|
| **Who** | To ask about a person | Who is your best friend? |
| **What** | To ask about a thing or information | What is your favorite color? |
| **Where** | To ask about a place | Where do you live? |
| **When** | To ask about time | When is your birthday? |
| **Why** | To ask about a reason | Why are you late? |
| **How** | To ask about a method or way | How do you make this dish? |
| **Which** | To ask about a choice | Which book do you want to read? |
| **Whose** | To ask about ownership | Whose bag is this? |

**What are Question Verbs?**

Question verbs are verbs used to make questions. They can be auxiliary verbs, modal verbs or main verbs.

**Auxiliary Verbs**

The table below shows the list of auxiliary verbs.

| Auxiliary Verb | Example Question |
|----------------|----------------------------------|
| Is | Is she coming to the party? |
| Am | Am I invited to the meeting? |
| Are | Are they going to the park? |
| Was | Was he at the office yesterday? |

| Have | Have they seen the movie yet? |
| Had | Had you completed the task? |
| Do | Do you like chocolate? |

##1. Question Making with Question Words

###1.1 With Question Verbs

One structure followed by questions using question words is:

**Question Word + Question Verb + rest of the question**

For example, look at the following sentence:

Person A: *Where did you go?*

Person B: I went to the park.

Where - question word

did - question verb

you go? - rest of the question

```
Other examples

What has happened?

Where are you going?

Who is calling?
 
When was it decided?
 
Why did she leave?

```

(**Note:** Question verbs can also be main verbs when used after question words. For example: Who called you yesterday? In this question, “called” is the main verb and is used after the question word “who” to form a question.)

###1.2 With Specific Topic and Question Verb

The structure followed to make questions with a specific topic and a question verb is:

**Question word + specific topic to be asked + question verb+ rest of the question**

For example, look at the following question:

**Which colour did you choose** for the wedding dress?

Which - question word

colour - specific topic

did - question verb

```

Other Examples

What book are you reading?
 
Which team will win the match?
 
Whose idea was this?
 
Which movie did you watch?
 
What solution has she proposed?

```

##Things to Remember Before Making Questions

- How to use auxiliary verbs and modal verbs for question making:

| **Category** | **Past** | **Present** | **Example Statement (Past)** | **Example Question (Past)** | **Example Statement (Present)** | **Example Question (Present)** |
|--------------------|-----------------------------|----------------------------------|-------------------------------------|--------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| **Doing/Action** (Actions performed by the subject) | Did | Do, Does | She played football. | Did she play football? | He likes chocolate. | Does he like chocolate? |
| **Thing** (Facts or states where the main verb remains the same) | Same verb (with ""did"") | Same verb (with ""do/does"") | He knew the answer. | Did he know the answer? | She knows the truth. | Does she know the truth? |
| **Possession** (Ownership) | Had | Has, Have | She had a car. | Did she have a car? | He has a book. | Does he have a book? |
| **Object/Person** (condition or state) | Was, Were | Is, Am, Are | They were at the park. | Were they at the park? | He is happy. | Is he happy? |

- The tense of the question verb depends on the tense of the main verb/ helping verb used in the sentence.

```

For example: She attended French classes last month.

Here, “attended” is the main verb which is in past tense. 

Therefore, the question verb should also be in past tense. 

Questions: What did she attend last month?

When did she attend French Classes?

What did she do last month?

```

- Certain pronouns and helping verbs also change form while making questions.

Look at the following conversations:

- You (speaker): How **are you**?

Person A (listener): **I am** fine

- You (speaker): What **were you** doing yesterday?

Person A (listener): **I was** watching a movie.

- You (speaker): Where does **your** father work?

Person A (listener): **My** father works in an IT company.
