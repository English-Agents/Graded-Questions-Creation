# Future Perfect

#Future Perfect Tense

##Definition

The future perfect tense is used to talk about something that **will be completed before a certain time in the future**. It helps us imagine a point in the future and look back at an action that will already be done by that time.

**”Will have”** is the key word here.

**Examples**

```
He will have traveled to five countries by the end of the year.
We will have saved enough money for the vacation by next summer.
You will have learned a lot by the time the course ends.
```
(**Note:** Future perfect tense always uses **“will have”** irrespective of whether the subject is singular or plural.)

##Structure

###Using “Will Have”

**Positive Sentences**

I/ you/ we/ any plural noun/singular noun/ they/that/ this/those/these/ he/ she/ it +** will have + verb (III form)**

**Examples**

```
She will have finished her homework by the time dinner is ready.
They will have reached the station before the train departs.
I will have completed the project by the end of the week.
```

**Negative Sentences**

I/ you/ we/ any plural noun/singular noun/ they/that/ this/those/these/ he/ she/ it +** will not have + verb (III form)**

**Examples**

```

The team will not have prepared the presentation by the time the meeting starts.

He will not have cleaned his room before his parents arrive.

We will not have finished packing our bags before the taxi arrives.

```

---

###Using “Will Have Been”

It is used in specific situations to talk about actions that will be completed by a certain point in the future and often emphasize **the result or completion of those actions**. Here, we focus on the action instead of the person performing the action. 

**Positive Sentences**

I/ you/ we/ any plural noun/singular noun/ they/that/ this/those/these/ he/ she/ it +** will have been + verb (III form)**

**Examples**

```

The work will have been finished before the deadline.

The reports will have been submitted by the end of the day.

The movie will have been watched by millions by the end of the month.

```

**Negative Sentences**

I/ you/ we/ any plural noun/singular noun/ they/that/ this/those/these/ he/ she/ it +** will not have been + verb (III form)**


**Examples**

```
The house will not have been cleaned by the time the guests arrive.

The work will not have been completed before the deadline.

The documents will not have been reviewed by the start of the meeting.
 ```
 
 ---
 
 ##Signal Words to Indicate Future Perfect Tense

| **Signal Word/Phrase** | **Example Sentence** |
|-------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------|
| **By** | She will have finished her homework by tomorrow. |
| **Before** | They will have left before the meeting starts. |
| **By the end of** | We will have completed the project by the end of the month. |
| **In (a period of time)** | He will have graduated in two years. |
| **No later than** | The report will have been submitted no later than Friday.|
| **By the time** | By the time you arrive, I will have cooked dinner. |
| **Within (a time frame)** | We will have fixed the issue within the next 24 hours.|

For sentences in the Future Perfect tense, the part of the sentence which **talks about time** (the part which is followed by the signal words) will usually be in **present tense**. 

For example, "By the time **you arrive**, I will have finished my work." Here, "you arrive" is in present tense.

##Future Perfect (will have + V3) v/s Future Possession 
| **Aspect** | **Future Perfect Tense (will have + V3)** | **Future Possession (will have + noun/ combination of noun and adjective)** |
|---------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------|
| **Definition** | Describes an action that will be completed by a specific future time. | Refers to something that someone will possess or own in the future. |
| **Structure** | Subject + will have + verb (III form). | Subject + will have + noun/ combination of noun and adjective. |
| **Example 1** | She will have finished the project by tomorrow. | She will have a new car by next month. |
| **Example 2** | They will have visited the museum by the time it closes. | They will have a bigger house in five years. |
| **Example 3** | He will have learned all the necessary skills by next year. | He will have a lot of experience after completing his degree. |
| **Example 4** | We will have completed the report before the deadline. | We will have enough savings to buy a house soon. |
| **Example 5** | The workers will have completed the construction by next summer. | The city will have a new park by next summer. |
